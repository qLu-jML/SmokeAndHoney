## Your Project's Funding Strengths

Before diving into opportunities, here's what makes Smoke & Honey attractive to funders:

- **Educational value** - Teaches real beekeeping biology, pollinator ecology, and agricultural science through gameplay. This opens doors to education-focused grants that pure entertainment games can't access.
- **Agricultural/environmental angle** - Pollinator conservation is a hot policy topic. USDA, state ag departments, and environmental nonprofits care about public awareness of bee biology.
- **Playable prototype** - Phase 1 is complete with 50+ scripts, 22+ scenes, and a working simulation engine. You're not pitching a concept -- you have a game.
- **Solo/small team** - Many grants specifically target solo devs and micro-studios.
- **Godot Engine (open source)** - No engine royalties eating into your budget. Funders like lean operations.

## Tier 1: LOW RISK, HIGH REWARD (Apply Immediately)

These require no financial commitment from you, no repayment, and accept projects at your stage of development.

### DANGEN Entertainment Indie Developer Grant
- **Amount:** Up to $50,000 USD (split across the pool)
- **Repayment:** None. No royalties, no strings.
- **Requirements:** Playable PC build, clear art direction, micro gameplay loop (you have all of this)
- **Disqualifier:** Cannot have existing third-party funding
- **Payout:** 50% upfront, 25% after each of two milestone builds
- **Apply:** https://grant.dangenentertainment.com/
- **Why you fit:** You have a working simulation with clear art direction and a complete core loop. This is exactly the stage they want.

### Itch.io "Itchfunding" (Soft Launch)
- **Amount:** Whatever people pay -- typically $5-50 per supporter
- **Risk:** Zero. You upload a playable demo, set a funding goal, and sell early access with no deadline.
- **How it works:** Create a product page on itch.io, set up a "Sale" with a dollar goal (displays a funding bar), price your demo at $5-10.
- **Why it works for you:** No pressure to finish. Supporters know they're backing a work-in-progress. If it makes $0, you've lost nothing but time setting up the page. If it makes $500-2000, that's grocery money while you develop.
- **Bonus:** Validates market interest, builds a mailing list, and gives you player feedback.
- **Site:** https://itch.io

### Ko-fi / Buy Me a Coffee
- **Amount:** One-time tips or monthly memberships ($3-10/month typical)
- **Risk:** Zero. No fees until you earn (Ko-fi takes 0% on one-time donations, 5% on memberships).
- **How it works:** Create a page, share devlog updates, let people tip you.
- **Why it works:** You're already generating interesting development content (hex-grid beekeeping simulation is inherently visual and fascinating). Post weekly devlog GIFs/screenshots.
- **Site:** https://ko-fi.com

## Tier 2: LOW RISK, MODERATE EFFORT (Apply Within 1-2 Months)

These require a proper application but have no financial risk.

### Indie Fund
- **Amount:** Varies (covers your stated budget needs)
- **Structure:** Loan -- but if your game doesn't earn enough within 2 years of release, you owe nothing.
- **Repayment:** Only from game revenue. 125% of loan amount, or 2 years post-release, whichever comes first. If the game earns $0, you pay $0.
- **Application:** Simple form at https://indie-fund.com/apply -- goes to their investor syndicate
- **Selectivity:** ~5 games/year funded. Worth the application effort though.
- **Why it's low risk:** You literally cannot lose money. Worst case: you get rejected. Second worst: you get funded, game doesn't sell, loan is forgiven.

### Xsolla Funding
- **Amount:** Varies -- connects you with 250+ investors and publishers
- **How it works:** Submit your game through their portal. They match you with interested investors during pitch events.
- **Risk:** Low -- it's essentially a matchmaking service. Terms depend on the investor.
- **Site:** https://xsolla.com/funding

### Gamedev Fund (Early-Stage Investment)
- **Focus:** Premium indie games for PC/consoles with a playable demo
- **What they provide:** Funding, knowledge, and contacts
- **Why you fit:** You have a playable demo and a clear premium PC game.

## Tier 3: EDUCATION & AGRICULTURE GRANTS (Higher Effort, Big Payoff)

Your game's educational angle in beekeeping and agricultural science opens doors most games can't access.

### USDA SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research)
- **Phase I:** Up to $175,000 for 8 months of R&D
- **Phase II:** Up to follow-on funding for commercialization
- **Requirement:** Must be a U.S. small business (sole proprietorship counts)
- **Angle:** Pitch Smoke & Honey as an agricultural education tool that teaches pollinator biology and hive management. Pollinator decline is a USDA priority area.
- **Site:** https://www.nifa.usda.gov/grants/programs/sbir-sttr
- **Effort level:** High -- requires a formal proposal. But $175K is life-changing money for a solo dev.

### Department of Education IES SBIR
- **Phase I:** Up to $200,000 for 8 months
- **Phase II:** Up to $900,000 for 2 years
- **Focus:** Commercially viable education technology products and games
- **Precedent:** They've funded games like "Reach for the Sun" (Filament Games) -- a plant biology simulation game very similar in spirit to Smoke & Honey.
- **Angle:** Smoke & Honey teaches authentic biological processes (queen laying patterns, varroa mite lifecycle, brood development) through observation-based gameplay. This is exactly what IES funds.
- **Site:** https://ies.ed.gov/funding/research/programs/small-business-innovation-research-sbir
- **Note:** You'd need to register as a small business (LLC or sole prop is fine).

### NEA Grants for Arts Projects
- **Amount:** $10,000 - $100,000
- **Catch:** Requires a nonprofit entity or fiscal sponsor (501(c)(3))
- **Workaround:** Partner with a local arts council or educational nonprofit as your fiscal sponsor. Many will do this for a 5-10% administrative fee.
- **Next deadline:** July 9, 2026 (FY2026 cycle)
- **Site:** https://www.arts.gov/grants/grants-for-arts-projects
- **Angle:** Interactive media / digital art. The NEA has funded video games before.

## Tier 4: REVENUE-GENERATING (When You're Ready)

### Steam Early Access
- **When:** Once Phase 2 (Harvest & Economy Loop) is complete -- you need a reason for players to keep playing.
- **Revenue split:** You keep 70% (Steam takes 30%)
- **Why wait:** Steam Early Access games with a core loop + economy loop have the strongest retention. Phase 1 alone (inspection without harvest/selling) would get poor reviews.
- **Target price:** $9.99-14.99 for Early Access, $19.99 at 1.0 launch
- **Stat to know:** 25% of Steam's top-selling games went through Early Access. But the median indie game on Steam earns very little -- marketing matters.

### Patreon (Monthly Memberships)
- **Fee:** 10% of earnings
- **Model:** $3/month for devlogs, $5/month for early builds, $10/month for design input
- **Why it works:** Your game has incredible devlog potential. Beekeeping simulation with real biology is fascinating content. People will pay to watch the process.
