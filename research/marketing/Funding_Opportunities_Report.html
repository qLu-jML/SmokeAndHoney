GRANT USDA SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research)

Up to $175,000 (Phase I, 8 months)
Phase IIFollow-on funding for commercialization RepaymentNone — this is a grant, not a loan RequirementMust be a U.S. small business (sole proprietorship counts) Your AngleAgricultural education tool teaching pollinator biology and hive management Linknifa.usda.gov/grants/programs/sbir-sttr

How to Apply — Step by Step

  1. Register a business entity: File a sole proprietorship or single-member LLC in your state. Cost is typically $50-150. This is required for all SBIR applications.
  2. Get a DUNS/UEI number: Register at SAM.gov (System for Award Management). This is free but can take 1-2 weeks to process. You need this before applying to any federal grant.
  3. Register on Grants.gov: Create an account at Grants.gov and link your SAM.gov registration.
  4. Find the current SBIR solicitation: Visit NIFA SBIR Topic Areas. Look for topics under "Small and Mid-Size Farms" or "Rural Development" that relate to agricultural education or pollinator health.
  5. Write your Phase I proposal (15-25 pages):
    • Problem statement: Pollinator decline is a critical agricultural issue. Public understanding of bee biology and hive management is low. Existing educational materials are passive (textbooks, videos) rather than experiential.
    • Innovation: Smoke & Honey uses a bio-accurate simulation (70,000-cell hive model, 14 cell states, population cohorts with mortality curves) to teach beekeeping through observation-based gameplay. Players learn real biology by doing, not reading.
    • Research plan: Phase I would fund completion of the harvest/economy loop and formal educational assessment (pre/post knowledge tests with target users).
    • Budget: Break down the $175K across 8 months: developer salary, software licenses, user testing, educational assessment consultant.
  6. Submit before the deadline: USDA SBIR typically has annual solicitation cycles. Check the current deadline on their site.
$175K is life-changing money for a solo developer. The application is work, but the grant is free money with no repayment. Pollinator decline is a USDA priority — your game directly addresses it.
