6. Is This a Passing Fascination?

Probably Not. Here's Why.

I've analyzed your codebase, your design documents, your changelog patterns, and your domain knowledge. Here's what I see:

  • People who are "just fascinated" don't write 85,000 words of systems documentation. They build a prototype, show it to friends, and move on. You designed a 34-quest economy with calibrated XP values and an 8-month agricultural calendar with custom month names.
  • People who are "just fascinated" don't iterate art 3 times. They use placeholder assets and say "I'll fix it later." You redrew hive sprites until the anatomy was correct and the perspective matched.
  • People who are "just fascinated" don't model 3D ellipsoid brood geometry. They'd use a flat grid and call it done. You implemented the real spatial biology because anything less would feel wrong to you.
  • The beekeeping knowledge isn't new to you. This isn't someone who discovered bees last month and got excited. The depth of domain knowledge suggests this has been a long interest that you're finally building something with.

Your risk isn't losing interest in beekeeping. Your risk is losing stamina during the unglamorous middle — the months of wiring UI elements, fixing save/load edge cases, and balancing economy numbers. That's the grind that kills indie projects, not lack of passion.

7. The Bottom Line

Is the game worth it? Yes. Is going all-in right now the smart move? Not yet.

What you've built in 6 days is genuinely impressive. The simulation is real, the design is deep, and the domain knowledge gives you an unfair advantage in a niche with proven demand (APICO: ~$730K gross) and virtually no competition at the simulation-depth level you're targeting.

But you're 20–25% done, you've never shipped a commercial game, and your current pace will burn you out in weeks. The smart move is the 90-Day Prove-It Plan: keep the chaplain salary, build Phase 2 on evenings and weekends, launch on itch.io, apply for grants, and evaluate the results with real data.

If you pass that test — and I think you will — then you'll quit your job with funding in hand, real player validation, and the proven knowledge that your interest survives the boring parts. That's not playing it safe. That's being strategic about something you care about enough to do right.
