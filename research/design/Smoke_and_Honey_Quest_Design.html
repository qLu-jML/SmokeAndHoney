Smoke & Honey — Complete quest lines for all 6 NPCs, mapped to the progression system, standing tiers, and location unlocks

Design Note — Quest Pacing: No NPC dumps their full quest chain at once. Quests are gated by level, season, standing, and prerequisite completion. A player who focuses only on one NPC will hit natural walls that push them toward other characters. This creates organic exploration of the full cast without forced branching.

Uncle Bob Mentor

Bob's quest chain is the backbone of Year 1. It doubles as the game's extended tutorial without ever feeling like one. Each quest teaches a core mechanic through a concrete task Bob needs help with. His chain unlocks the Timber & Woodlot apiary via Lloyd Petersen (introduced through Bob).

Q1: First Light → Q2: Reading the Room → Q3: Sugar Water Days → Q4: The Girls Are Building → Q5: First Pull → Q6: Battening Down → Q7: Lloyd's Lot → Q8: Spring Count
Q1: First Light
Level 1 Early Spring Y1 Tutorial

Uncle Bob hands Eli the smoker, veil, and hive tool. Together they open the surviving hive for the first time. Bob walks Eli through lighting the smoker, approaching the hive, and pulling the first frame.

Objective: Light the smoker, open the surviving hive, pull and inspect 3 frames, identify the queen or fresh eggs.

+25 XP +15 Standing Unlocks: Smoker, Veil, Hive Tool (permanent equipment) Knowledge Log: "Opening a Hive"
Q2: Reading the Room
Level 1 Spring Y1 Tutorial After Q1

Bob asks Eli to do a full inspection solo while he watches from the tailgate. "Tell me what you see. Don't tell me what you think—tell me what you see." Teaches the observation-over-stats philosophy.

Objective: Complete a full 10-frame inspection unassisted. Correctly identify brood pattern quality (good/patchy/failing), estimate bee population (small/medium/strong), note any anomalies.

+25 XP +15 Standing Knowledge Log: "Reading a Brood Pattern" Unlocks: Weekly inspection reminders in journal
Q3: Sugar Water Days
Level 1 Spring Y1 Tutorial After Q2

The surviving colony is weak and stores are thin. Bob shows Eli how to mix 1:1 sugar syrup and set up a feeder. "You're not feeding them forever. You're buying them time until the dandelions open."

Objective: Purchase sugar from Tanner's Supply. Mix syrup at the workbench. Install the feeder on the surviving hive. Check and refill the feeder 3 times over the next 2 weeks.

+25 XP +15 Standing Knowledge Log: "Emergency Feeding" Unlocks: Feeder crafting recipe at workbench
Q4: The Girls Are Building
Level 1 Late Spring Y1 NPC Quest After Q3 + colony reaches medium strength

The colony is growing. Bob notices they're running out of room. "See how they're building burr comb on top? That's them telling you they need space." Time to add the first super.

Objective: Clean and prep one of the dead hive supers at the workbench. Install it on the surviving hive. Verify within 1 week that bees are drawing comb on the new frames.

+50 XP +20 Standing Knowledge Log: "When to Add Space" Unlocks: Equipment cleaning/repair at workbench
Q5: First Pull
Level 1 Summer Y1 NPC Quest After Q4 + at least 3 capped honey frames

The first honey harvest. Bob brings out the hand-crank extractor. "This is the part everyone thinks they're here for. But the honey's not the point—the honey is proof the bees are doing well." He lets Eli do the uncapping.

Objective: Select frames with ≥80% capping. Uncap, extract, and strain at least 5 lbs of honey. Jar the harvest. Bring one jar to Uncle Bob.

+75 XP +30 Standing Cash: First honey sale value Knowledge Log: "Harvest & Extraction" Unlocks: Saturday Market selling (Frank Fischbach introduction)
Q6: Battening Down
Level 1 Fall Y1 Seasonal / NPC After Q5

Fall is coming. Bob teaches winter preparation: mite treatment, weight estimation, mouse guards, moisture management. "Beekeeping isn't about summer. Any fool can keep bees in July. The real work is getting them to March."

Objective: Complete varroa treatment on all hives. Install mouse guards. Verify stores are ≥60 lbs per hive (or feed to target). Add ventilation board. Report readiness to Bob.

+100 XP +30 Standing Knowledge Log: "Winterization" Unlocks: Bob mentions his friend Lloyd Petersen who has timber land
Q7: Lloyd's Lot
Level 2 Spring Y2 NPC Quest After Q6 + Level 2 reached + surviving winter

Bob introduces Eli to Lloyd Petersen, an old friend who owns timber and woodlot acreage. Lloyd's amenable to hives on his land but needs the clearing prepped. Bob can't do the work himself anymore—"My back says I'm fifty-eight. My heart says maybe not even that."

Objective: Visit Lloyd's property with Bob. Walk the site. Pay the $200 clearing fee to Lloyd. Clear and prep the apiary site (3 in-game tasks over 1 week). Place first hive at Timber & Woodlot.

Major Unlock: Timber & Woodlot Apiary — New forage zone with basswood, tulip poplar, and wildflower access. Up to 4 hive slots.
+100 XP +40 Standing Cost: $200 site clearing fee Knowledge Log: "Apiary Site Selection"
Q8: Spring Count
Level 2–3 Spring Y3 NPC Quest After Q7 + 2nd winter survived

Two years in. Bob and Eli walk the apiaries together. Bob lets Eli lead the inspection while he watches. At the end he says: "I think you know more than I did at this point. Don't tell anyone I said that." This is the capstone—the student surpassing the mentor.

Objective: Inspect all hives across both apiaries. Report winter survival rate to Bob. If ≥80% survived: Bob's chain completes with full dialogue. If <80%: Bob gives encouragement and the quest completes with modified dialogue.

+150 XP +50 Standing Bob's chain complete — unlocks recurring mentorship visits (bi-weekly, +20 XP each, indefinite) Knowledge Log: "The Mentor's Approval"
Design Note — Bob's Recurring Presence: After the chain completes, Bob doesn't vanish. He remains available for bi-weekly visits (+20–25 XP), appears at the Crossroads Diner on Tuesdays, and has seasonal dialogue that reflects his health and the weather. His presence is permanent and evolving. He just stops being the quest driver and becomes the emotional anchor.

Darlene Kowalski Master Beekeeper

Darlene is the safety net and the standard. Her quests focus on quality, queen management, and the deeper craft of beekeeping. She's visible from the start (fence line neighbor) but her quest chain activates through observation and need—the player comes to her when something goes wrong, or when they're ready to go deeper.

Q1: Over the Fence → Q2: The Marked Queen → Q3: When Good Queens Go Bad → Q4: Three Hive Walk → Q5: The Old Way → Q6: Passing It Forward
Q1: Over the Fence
Level 1 Spring Y1 NPC Quest

Eli notices Darlene working her hives on the adjacent property. She waves him over. "You're Bob's nephew? He told me you were coming. Let me show you something." She demonstrates how her established hives look compared to Eli's recovering colony—the contrast is educational.

Objective: Visit Darlene's property. Observe her inspect one of her hives. Answer 3 observation questions correctly (brood pattern, population estimate, queen status).

+50 XP +20 Standing Knowledge Log: "What a Healthy Hive Looks Like" Unlocks: Can visit Darlene's property anytime for dialogue
Q2: The Marked Queen
Level 1–2 Summer Y1 NPC Quest After Q1 + spotted queen 3 times

Darlene teaches queen marking. "You need to know who's in charge. And more importantly, you need to know when she's been replaced." She provides a marking pen and demonstrates on one of her queens.

Objective: Find and mark the queen in your surviving hive using the international color system. Must complete within a single inspection without losing her.

+75 XP +25 Standing Item: Queen marking pen (permanent tool) Knowledge Log: "Queen Identification & Marking"
Q3: When Good Queens Go Bad
Level 2 Y2 (triggered by queen failure event) NPC Quest / Challenge After Q2 + queen failure in any hive

A queen fails—poor brood pattern, multiple eggs per cell, or she's simply gone. Darlene walks Eli through emergency requeening. "Every beekeeper loses a queen. It's what you do in the next 48 hours that separates the hobbyists from the beekeepers."

Objective: Identify the failing/missing queen. Order a replacement through Tanner's Supply (or accept one from Darlene if standing ≥250). Install the new queen. Verify acceptance after 3 days (check for released cage, no queen cells built).

+100 XP +30 Standing Knowledge Log: "Emergency Requeening" Unlocks: Queen sourcing from Darlene at high relationship
Q4: Three Hive Walk
Level 2–3 Spring Y2 or Y3 NPC Quest After Q3 + managing ≥4 hives

Darlene invites Eli to help inspect all three of her hives in one session. She grades his technique and observation skills. "You're looking, but are you seeing? Tell me which hive you'd split and why."

Objective: Inspect all 3 of Darlene's hives. For each, record: queen status, brood quality, population strength, varroa load estimate, and stores estimate. Correctly identify which hive is the best split candidate.

+100 XP +35 Standing Knowledge Log: "Comparative Hive Assessment" Unlocks: Darlene's advanced dialogue on queen genetics
Q5: The Old Way
Level 3–4 Y3–Y4 NPC Quest After Q4 + Level 3

Darlene shares her grandmother's approach to queen selection—observational genetics before there were testing kits. "We didn't have DNA analysis. We had eyes, patience, and three generations of notes." She teaches Eli to evaluate queen quality by observable brood traits.

Objective: Over 4 inspections across 2 months, evaluate queen performance in 3+ hives using Darlene's criteria: brood compactness, pattern consistency, temperament during inspection, honey production rate. Rank your queens and present your assessment to Darlene.

+125 XP +40 Standing Knowledge Log: "Observational Queen Assessment" Unlocks: Preferred queen traits visible in inspection UI
Q6: Passing It Forward
Level 4 Y4+ NPC Quest After Q5 + Level 4 + queen rearing unlocked

Darlene is slowing down. She asks Eli to raise a queen from her best colony's genetics—one she can put in her hive, so her grandmother's line continues. "I can't do the grafting anymore. My hands aren't steady enough. But you can."

Objective: Graft queen cells from Darlene's best colony. Successfully raise a mated queen. Install her in one of Darlene's hives. Verify laying within 2 weeks.

Darlene's Chain Complete — Darlene defers to Eli on technique questions going forward. Permanent: emergency queen sourcing from Darlene at no cost (once per season).
+150 XP +60 Standing Knowledge Log: "The Legacy of a Line" Unlocks: Darlene's queen line available for grafting (premium genetics)

Frank Fischbach Market Vendor

Frank is the economic backbone. His quests teach the market side of beekeeping: presentation, consistency, reputation, and scaling. He's the first NPC who treats Eli like a business partner, not a student. His approval unlocks premium pricing and county fair access.

Q1: First Jar → Q2: The Saturday Regulars → Q3: Label and Shelf → Q4: Bulk Order → Q5: County Fair
Q1: First Jar
Level 1 Summer Y1 NPC Quest After Bob Q5 (First Pull)

Bob sends Eli to the Saturday Market with a few jars. Frank takes one look and gives honest feedback: "Honey's good. Presentation needs work. Come back next week with a label and I'll give you a spot."

Objective: Bring at least 3 jars of honey to the Saturday Market. Speak with Frank. Sell at least 1 jar (any price).

+50 XP +15 Standing Cash: Market price for sold jars Unlocks: Regular Saturday Market attendance
Q2: The Saturday Regulars
Level 1–2 Late Summer Y1 NPC Quest After Q1 + attended 4 markets

Frank notices Eli's been consistent. "People are asking for you. That's worth more than the honey." He teaches the market economy: how regulars build baseline income, how variety increases average sale, and why showing up matters more than having the best product.

Objective: Attend 4 consecutive Saturday Markets. Sell at least 2 different products (honey + one wax product or herb bundle). Total revenue ≥$50 across the 4 markets.

+75 XP +25 Standing Unlocks: Frank's premium pricing dialogue (+2% base price ongoing) Knowledge Log: "Market Economics"
Q3: Label and Shelf
Level 2 Y2 NPC Quest After Q2 + Standing ≥100 (Known)

Frank offers Eli a permanent shelf spot at the market—not just a folding table. "Real shelf. Your name on it. But I need consistent supply: minimum 6 jars every Saturday through the season. Can you do that?"

Objective: Maintain ≥6 jars of honey stocked at Frank's shelf for 6 consecutive market weeks. No gaps.

Premium Shelf Space — Permanent market presence. +5% sale prices. Products sell even if Eli doesn't attend in person.
+100 XP +35 Standing Permanent +5% market price bonus Knowledge Log: "Building a Brand"
Q4: Bulk Order
Level 3 Y3 NPC Quest After Q3 + Level 3 + Honey House built

Frank comes to Eli with a proposition: the Cedar Bend General Store wants to stock local honey year-round. "They want 20 lbs a month, minimum. Steady contract. Real money. But you miss a delivery and they find someone else."

Objective: Fulfill a contract for 20 lbs of honey per month for 3 consecutive months. Honey must be ≤18% moisture (quality standard).

+125 XP +45 Standing Ongoing contract: Guaranteed monthly income Knowledge Log: "Wholesale & Contracts"
Q5: County Fair
Level 3–4 Summer Y3–Y4 NPC Quest / Challenge After Q4 + Standing ≥500 (Respected) Standing 500+

Frank sponsors Eli's entry in the Millhaven County Fair honey competition. "Time to see how you stack up against the whole county. I've been entering my pickles for twelve years. Never won. But the trying—that's the point."

Objective: Submit your best honey for judging. Entry judged on: clarity, moisture (≤17.8%), flavor profile, and presentation. Placement depends on actual honey quality stats.

+150 XP Standing: +50 (1st), +25 (2nd), +10 (3rd), +5 (participation) 1st Place: Rare queen voucher + county recognition Knowledge Log: "Competition Honey"

Dr. Ellen Harwick Extension Agent

Ellen is the science. She enters the story when things go wrong and stays for mastery-level depth. Her quests are the most technically demanding and represent the transition from practical beekeeper to scientifically literate apiarist. She gates the late-game mastery content.

Q1: The County Call → Q2: Under the Microscope → Q3: The IPM Protocol → Q4: Sentinel Hive → Q5: The Certification
Q1: The County Call
Level 2 Y2 (triggered by first disease/pest event) NPC Quest / Challenge

A varroa spike or suspicious brood triggers a visit from Dr. Harwick. She doesn't sugarcoat it. "I'm Dr. Harwick, extension service. Your mite count is above threshold. Let's talk about what that means and what we're going to do about it."

Objective: Perform an alcohol wash mite count with Dr. Harwick observing. Record the result. Follow her treatment recommendation. Recount after treatment to verify efficacy.

+75 XP +20 Standing Knowledge Log: "Varroa Threshold Management" Unlocks: Can request lab analysis from Dr. Harwick anytime
Q2: Under the Microscope
Level 2–3 Y2–Y3 NPC Quest After Q1 + 2nd pest/disease event

Dr. Harwick invites Eli to the extension office to see lab analysis firsthand. "You're past the point where 'the bees look sick' is good enough. Let me show you what sick actually looks like." She teaches diagnostic sampling.

Objective: Collect samples from 3 hives (bee samples + brood samples). Deliver to Dr. Harwick's office. Review results together. Correctly identify which hive has the issue and propose a treatment plan.

+100 XP +30 Standing Knowledge Log: "Laboratory Diagnostics" Unlocks: Sample collection kit (permanent tool), disease detail in inspection UI
Q3: The IPM Protocol
Level 3 Y3 NPC Quest After Q2 + Level 3

Dr. Harwick proposes a structured Integrated Pest Management protocol for Eli's operation. "You're big enough now that reactive treatment isn't sufficient. I want you running a calendar: monitor, threshold, treat, verify. Every hive, every month."

Objective: Implement a full IPM season: monthly mite counts on all hives, treatment when above threshold, post-treatment verification. Maintain records for one full active season (spring through fall). No hive should exceed 3% mite load at any monthly check.

+125 XP +40 Standing Knowledge Log: "Integrated Pest Management" Unlocks: IPM calendar overlay in journal, treatment reminder system
Q4: Sentinel Hive
Level 4 Y4 Mastery Quest After Q3 + Level 4 + Observation Hive built

Dr. Harwick asks Eli to designate one hive as a sentinel—an untreated monitoring hive that provides early warning data for the county. "It's risky. You'll lose the colony if mites spike. But the data you generate helps every beekeeper in Millhaven."

Objective: Designate a hive as a sentinel. Perform weekly mite counts without treating. Report data to Dr. Harwick monthly. Maintain the sentinel for one full season. If mite counts exceed 5%, Dr. Harwick will advise whether to terminate the experiment or continue.

+200 XP +50 Standing Knowledge Log: "Sentinel Monitoring & Regional Data" Unlocks: County mite forecast data in journal (regional context for your counts)
Q5: The Certification
Level 5 Y5+ Mastery Quest After Q4 + Level 5

Dr. Harwick recommends Eli for the state's Master Beekeeper certification program. "I don't recommend people lightly. You've earned this." The exam covers everything: biology, management, diseases, economics, and a practical demonstration.

Objective: Pass a multi-part assessment: written knowledge check (15 questions from Knowledge Log), practical inspection observed by Dr. Harwick (must identify 5 specific conditions), and present a season management plan for a 15+ hive operation.

Dr. Harwick's Chain Complete — Master Beekeeper certification. Permanent: advanced diagnostic detail in all inspections. Dr. Harwick refers other beekeepers to Eli for advice (new dialogue encounters).
+200 XP +75 Standing Title: "Certified Master Beekeeper" Knowledge Log: "Master Certification"

Walt & Kacey Harmon Farm Neighbors

The Harmon chain is primarily through Kacey, who bridges the gap between agriculture and beekeeping. This chain unlocks the Harmon Farm / Orchard apiary—a major forage zone expansion. It also explores the tension between conventional farming and pollinator-friendly practices. Walt comes around slowly; the player earns his respect through results, not arguments.

Q1: The Dead Bees → Q2: Kacey's Idea → Q3: The Test Strip → Q4: Pollination Deal → Q5: Cover Crop Season → Q6: Walt Comes Around
Q1: The Dead Bees
Level 2 Summer Y2 (pesticide event trigger) Challenge / NPC Quest

Eli finds a carpet of dead bees in front of a hive after the Harmon Farm sprays during bloom. This is the pesticide event—the first time Eli confronts the reality that his bees don't exist in isolation. Bob suggests talking to the Harmons, but warns: "Don't go angry. Go informed."

Objective: Document the dead bees (inspection record). Visit the Harmon Farm. Speak with Walt (initially dismissive). Meet Kacey, who's sympathetic. Provide evidence of the bee kill.

+75 XP +15 Standing Knowledge Log: "Pesticide Impact on Pollinators" Unlocks: Kacey Harmon as an interactable NPC
Q2: Kacey's Idea
Level 2 Fall Y2 NPC Quest After Q1

Kacey reaches out. She studied agronomy and has been pushing Walt toward pollinator-safe spray timing. "My dad's not a bad guy. He just farms the way his dad farmed. Help me show him there's a better way that doesn't cost him yield."

Objective: Meet with Kacey at the farm. Review the spray schedule together. Propose a modified timing (spray at dusk, not dawn; avoid bloom windows). Present the plan to Walt. He agrees to try it for one season.

+75 XP +25 Standing Knowledge Log: "Working with Conventional Farmers" Unlocks: Modified spray schedule (reduced pesticide events on Harmon Farm)
Q3: The Test Strip
Level 2–3 Spring Y3 NPC Quest After Q2

Kacey convinces Walt to let her plant a test strip of cover crops along the south fence—clover and buckwheat between the corn rows. She needs Eli's help. "If we can show Dad the bees use it and his yield doesn't drop, he'll let us do more."

Objective: Help Kacey plant the cover crop test strip (3 in-game tasks). Monitor bee activity on the strip over 2 months. Document forage visits from your hives (entrance observation sessions). Report findings to Kacey.

+100 XP +30 Standing Knowledge Log: "Cover Crops & Pollinator Forage" Kacey friendship level 2
Q4: Pollination Deal
Level 2–3 Late Spring Y3 NPC Quest After Q3 (Kacey friendship level 3)

The test strip worked. Kacey proposes a formal arrangement: Eli places hives on the Harmon Farm for pollination services, and in exchange gets access to the orchard and field edges as a forage zone. No money changes hands—it's a service swap.

Objective: Negotiate terms with Kacey (and Walt, who's now cautiously interested). Move 2–3 hives to the Harmon Farm apiary site. Verify bees are foraging on farm crops within 2 weeks.

Major Unlock: Harmon Farm / Orchard Apiary — New forage zone with orchard bloom, field flowers, and cover crop access. Up to 4 hive slots. Free (service exchange).
+125 XP +40 Standing Harmon Farm apiary unlocked Knowledge Log: "Pollination Services"
Q5: Cover Crop Season
Level 3 Y3–Y4 Seasonal / NPC Quest After Q4

With the pollination deal working, Kacey pushes to expand cover crops across more of the farm. She needs Eli to demonstrate the economic value: more bees, more pollination, higher crop yield. It's a season-long experiment.

Objective: Maintain hives at Harmon Farm for a full growing season. Track honey production from farm-adjacent hives separately. Provide Kacey with a season summary: pollination coverage, honey yield, crop proximity data.

+100 XP +35 Standing Expanded cover crops on Harmon Farm (improved forage quality permanently) Knowledge Log: "Agriculture-Apiculture Symbiosis"
Q6: Walt Comes Around
Level 3–4 Y4 NPC Quest After Q5

Walt seeks Eli out at the market. He doesn't say much—he's not that kind of man—but he says enough. "Kacey's crop numbers are up 12% on the fields where your girls are working. I'm not going to pretend I understand it. But I understand 12%." He offers to expand the apiary on his land.

Objective: Accept Walt's offer. Expand the Harmon Farm apiary to its full capacity. Place additional hives. Walt provides site improvements at his own cost.

Harmon Chain Complete — Full Harmon Farm apiary (expanded to 6 hive slots). Permanent: no pesticide events on Harmon Farm land. Walt becomes a background ally—occasional dialogue, seasonal crop updates.
+150 XP +50 Standing Expanded apiary: 6 hive slots at Harmon Farm Knowledge Log: "Earning Trust Through Results"

Cedar Valley Beekeepers Association Community

The CVBA chain is the community track. It builds standing steadily and unlocks the River Bottom apiary—the premium late-game forage location. These quests require sustained community engagement rather than individual skill.

Q1: The New Kid → Q2: Show and Tell → Q3: The Equipment Swap → Q4: River Bottom
Q1: The New Kid
Level 1–2 Any (after first meeting) NPC Quest

Eli attends his first CVBA meeting at the Grange Hall. He's the youngest person there by decades. Some members are welcoming, some skeptical. The president asks everyone to introduce themselves and share their biggest beekeeping challenge this season.

Objective: Attend 3 CVBA monthly meetings. Listen to the speaker at each. Introduce yourself at the first meeting. Share an observation or question at the third meeting.

+75 XP +25 Standing Knowledge Log entries: 3 (one per speaker topic) Unlocks: CVBA bulletin board quests (small community tasks)
Q2: Show and Tell
Level 2–3 Y2–Y3 NPC Quest After Q1 + Standing ≥250 (Trusted) Standing 250+

The CVBA president asks Eli to present at a meeting. "The members want to hear from someone who started from scratch. Tell them about your first year." Public speaking—a different kind of challenge.

Objective: Prepare a presentation (select 3 topics from your Knowledge Log to present). Deliver it at the monthly meeting. Answer 2 questions from members.

+100 XP +40 Standing Knowledge Log: "Teaching What You Know" Unlocks: Queen trades and equipment exchanges at CVBA meetings
Q3: The Equipment Swap
Level 3 Y3 NPC Quest After Q2

The CVBA organizes an annual equipment swap and honey tasting. Members bring surplus equipment, queens, and honey. Eli can trade, buy, and sell—and the event builds the community connections needed for the River Bottom proposal.

Objective: Donate or trade at least 2 items at the equipment swap. Bring honey for the tasting. Network with 3 CVBA members (specific dialogue interactions).

+100 XP +45 Standing Equipment acquired at swap prices (40% below retail) Unlocks: River Bottom proposal discussion with CVBA leadership
Q4: River Bottom
Level 3 Y3–Y4 NPC Quest After Q3 + Standing ≥500 (Respected) + Level 3 Standing 500+

The CVBA has access to River Bottom land—premium bottomland forage with willow, sycamore, and wild meadow. It's been fallow since the last beekeeper retired. The association votes to offer Eli the site, contingent on a seasonal lease and site prep commitment.

Objective: Attend the CVBA vote meeting. Accept the River Bottom lease ($500 for site prep + seasonal fee). Prepare the site (3 tasks over 2 weeks). Place first hive at River Bottom. Maintain at least 2 hives there through the first season.

Major Unlock: River Bottom Apiary — Premium forage zone. Wildflower meadow, riparian corridor, bottomland diversity. Up to 6 hive slots. Best varietal honey potential in the game.
+150 XP +60 Standing Cost: $500 site prep + seasonal lease River Bottom apiary unlocked Knowledge Log: "Community Land Stewardship"
Design Note — CVBA Recurring Content: Beyond the quest chain, CVBA monthly meetings continue indefinitely. Each meeting has a unique speaker topic drawn from the Knowledge Log system. Attending provides +30 XP and a Knowledge Log entry. The meetings also serve as a social hub where other NPCs occasionally appear with one-off dialogue.

Quest Interconnection Map

No quest chain exists in isolation. The design creates natural cross-NPC dependencies that encourage the player to engage with the full cast without forced branching.

Design Note — Failure as Progression: Quest failures don't lock the player out permanently. A failed seasonal goal delays progression by one season and costs standing, but the quest reappears. NPC quest failures delay the specific chain by one season and reduce standing with that NPC. The design ensures the player always has a path forward—just a slower one. This mirrors real beekeeping: a bad year sets you back, but it doesn't end you.

Design Note — XP Balance: The 3,425 XP from all NPC quest chains represents roughly 14% of the 24,000 XP needed for Master Beekeeper. This is intentional. NPC quests are impactful milestones but the majority of XP comes from the steady rhythms of beekeeping itself: inspections, harvests, mite counts, market days, and the quiet accumulation of showing up. The quests provide structure and narrative. The bees provide the progression.
