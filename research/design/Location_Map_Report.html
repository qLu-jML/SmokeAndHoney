Executive Summary

This report catalogs every distinct scene map required to bring Smoke & Honey to life as specified in the GDD. It evaluates the current codebase against those requirements, draws comparative lessons from Stardew Valley and Harvest Moon, and provides suggested layouts for each map along with a world connection diagram showing how players navigate between locations.

The GDD specifies 6 primary outdoor locations (Home Property, County Road, Cedar Bend town, Timber & Woodlot, Harmon Farm, River Bottom) plus 1 bonus location (Town Garden), which expand into 18 total scene maps when counting all required interiors and sub-areas. Of these 18, the current build has 7 partially built and 11 not yet started.

Scope at a Glance: 18 scene maps. 7 outdoor environments. 9 interiors. 2 sub-areas. 25 total hive slots spread across 5 apiary locations. Each outdoor scene requires 4-6 seasonal variants (palette swaps + overlay layers). The world is compact by design -- intimate, not sprawling. Every location earns its place through gameplay function and narrative weight.

Key Lessons for Smoke & Honey

Lesson 1 -- Every location needs a daily-visit reason. Stardew's shops have NPC schedules that change daily. Harvest Moon's farm animals need daily care. S&H's locations should each have a reason to return: the home apiary for inspections, the diner for weather/Bob dialogue, the market for weekly sales, remote apiaries for management. Locations without daily hooks feel dead.

Lesson 2 -- Transitional spaces matter. Both games use corridor scenes (bus stop, backwoods, paths) to make the world feel physically connected. The County Road serves this role perfectly. It should never feel like a loading screen.

Lesson 3 -- Progressive revelation is critical. Both games gate locations behind friendship, money, or story progress. S&H's unlock chain (Home free, Town Garden Y1, Timber Y2, Orchard Y2-3, River Y3) matches this pattern well. The player should always have one locked location visible on the map as motivation.

Lesson 4 -- 12-20 total scenes is the sweet spot. Stardew has ~20. Harvest Moon FoMT has ~12. S&H's 18 is right in the middle -- enough variety without spreading content thin.

Lesson 5 -- Interiors should feel lived-in. The best farm sim interiors (Stardew's saloon, HM's supermarket) have specific character. Smoke & Honey's diner is already the strongest interior -- it should be the template for all others.

Recommended Build Order

Organized by gameplay dependency -- what the player needs first, built first. Each phase should result in a playable vertical slice.

PhaseScenes to BuildWhy This Order
Phase 1 (Core Loop)Complete Home Property (main.tscn polish), Shed Interior, House Interior (flesh out)The player spends 60-70% of Year 1 here. Hive inspection, crafting, Knowledge Log -- all core loop activities happen on these 3 scenes.
Phase 2 (World Access)Polish County Road, complete Cedar Bend main street, Back FieldGives the player somewhere to go. Market sales, NPC interactions, and long-term planting investments become available.
Phase 3 (Town Depth)Complete Diner, Tanner's, Post Office interiors. Build Dr. Harwick's Office, Grange Hall.NPC quest chains require these interiors. Bob's diner visits, Frank's market quests, Ellen's diagnostics, CVBA meetings.
Phase 4 (Year 2 Content)Timber & Woodlot, Town GardenFirst expansion locations. Timber is the most atmospheric -- build it when you have seasonal systems working. Town Garden is small and fast.
