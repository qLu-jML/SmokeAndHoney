# Smoke & Honey - Kickstarter Trailer Outline
## Core Loop Focus | ~60-90 seconds

**Music:** mainTheme.mp3 (full track, fade at end)
**Tone:** Cozy, rhythmic, "a day in the life" — show the satisfying loop
**Recording tool:** OBS or Godot's built-in screen capture (F12 in editor)

---

## PRE-RECORDING CHECKLIST

Before you hit record, set up a save file that's in good shape:

- Mid-spring or early summer (peak season, flowers visible, good weather)
- A few hives with capped honey frames ready to pull
- Smoker and bee suit in inventory
- Some jars already bottled (so the sell screen has stock)
- Dev mode ON for Level 5 inspection visuals (shows off the full frame detail)
- HUD visible with weather, time, energy

## EDITING GUIDE

### Software
Free options that work well for this length:
- **DaVinci Resolve** (free, professional — recommended if you have time to learn it)
- **Kdenlive** (free, simpler, Linux-friendly)
- **CapCut** (free, very beginner-friendly, good text tools)

### Timing & Rhythm
- Total target: **60-90 seconds** (Kickstarter sweet spot — people drop off hard after 90s)
- The main theme is your metronome — cut on musical beats when possible
- Speed up ALL walking/transition footage to 1.5-2x
- Keep gameplay moments (minigames, inspection, selling) at normal speed or slightly sped up
- Text cards should land on musical phrases (downbeats)

### Addressing the Art Situation
You have two good options:

**Option A — Lean into it (recommended):**
Add a brief text overlay early in the video:
> "Gameplay footage — final pixel art in development"

This is honest, common for Kickstarter game trailers, and actually works in your favor because it shows the systems are REAL and WORKING. Backers fund potential, and functional gameplay is more convincing than pretty art with no mechanics.

**Option B — Minimal acknowledgment:**
Just include "Art: [Pixel Artist] — Coming Soon" on the closing card and let the gameplay speak for itself. If the core loop is satisfying to watch, people won't fixate on placeholder sprites.

### What to Emphasize
The trailer should make viewers feel three things:
1. **"This is real"** — Functional gameplay, not a concept video
2. **"This has depth"** — The inspection system, cell-level simulation, multiple minigames
3. **"This is cozy"** — Music, pacing, seasonal world, small-town setting

### What to Avoid
- Don't show menus, settings, or debug output
- Don't show long walks between locations (speed them up)
- Don't try to explain game mechanics with text — let the visuals do it
- Don't show any rough edges (UI bugs, empty areas, placeholder text)
- Don't exceed 90 seconds — shorter is better for Kickstarter

## RECORDING TIPS

- **Resolution:** Record at 1920x1080 if possible. Kickstarter embeds at 16:9.
- **Clean HUD:** Make sure the HUD looks good — weather icon, time, energy bar all visible and working. Hide any debug text.
- **Consistent save:** Use ONE save file for all recording so inventory/money/season are consistent.
- **Multiple takes:** Record each section 3-4 times. You want options in the edit.
- **Capture audio separately:** Record with game audio ON (for SFX reference) but plan to replace it entirely with the main theme in post. The bee_buzz_loop.wav could be mixed in subtly under the music during the inspection sequence.
