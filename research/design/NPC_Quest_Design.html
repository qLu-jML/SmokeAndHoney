NPC 01 — Home Property

Uncle Bob — Robert "Bob" Whitfield

Experienced local beekeeper. Family friend. The reason Eli is here at all.

Location: Home Property / Crossroads Diner (Tuesdays) Years: 1 – 5 Domain: Mentorship, Craft, Legacy Rewards: Equipment, XP, Apiary Access
Narrative Arc

Year 1: Bob is the guide — Eli is anxious, uncertain, learning everything. Year 2: Bob steps back as Eli grows more confident; Bob mentions his health in passing. Year 3: Bob becomes the emotional anchor rather than the instructor. Years 4–5: Eli carries the operation forward as Bob's capacity diminishes. The arc ends not with drama but with quiet dignity — a man watching something he loves continue without him.

Q1
First Light
Unlock: Game start — Year 1, Early Spring
"Don't overthink it. Light the smoker. Follow me. Ask questions later."
Objectives
  • Light the smoker using dry fuel (Bob demonstrates technique)
  • Open the surviving hive with Bob watching
  • Pull and examine 3 frames without dropping or crushing bees
  • Locate the queen or identify fresh eggs to confirm her presence
Rewards
+25 XP +15 Standing Smoker (permanent) Veil (permanent) Hive Tool (permanent) Log: "Opening a Hive"
Q2
Reading the Room
Unlock: After Q1 — Spring Y1
"Tell me what you see. Don't tell me what you think I want to hear."
Objectives
  • Complete a full 10-frame inspection without Bob assisting
  • Correctly identify brood pattern quality (solid, spotty, or concerning)
  • Estimate bee population within one category (light/medium/strong)
  • Note at least one anomaly or point of interest in the inspection log
Rewards
+25 XP +15 Standing Weekly Inspection Reminders Log: "Reading a Brood Pattern"
Q3
Sugar Water Days
Unlock: After Q2 — Spring Y1
"You're not feeding them forever. You're buying them time until the dandelions open."
Objectives
  • Purchase a bag of sugar from Tanner's Supply
  • Mix 1:1 syrup ratio at the workbench (first crafting task)
  • Install the entrance feeder on the hive
  • Check and refill the feeder 3 times over 2 in-game weeks
Rewards
+25 XP +15 Standing Feeder Crafting Recipe Log: "Emergency Feeding"
Q4
The Girls Are Building
Unlock: After Q3 + colony reaches medium strength
"See how they're building burr comb on top? That's them telling you they need space."
Objectives
  • Clean and scrape burr comb from one of the empty hive supers at the workbench
  • Install the cleaned super on the growing hive
  • Verify bees begin drawing foundation comb within 7 in-game days
  • Report the colony's expansion progress to Bob
Rewards
+50 XP +20 Standing Equipment Cleaning/Repair Skill Log: "When to Add Space"
Q5
First Pull
Unlock: After Q4 + 3 or more capped honey frames
"This is the part everyone thinks they're here for. But the honey's not the point — the honey is proof the bees are doing well."
Objectives
  • Select frames with at least 80% capping coverage
  • Uncap, extract, and strain a minimum of 5 lbs of honey
  • Fill and label at least 2 jars properly
  • Bring one jar to Bob — he inspects the color and tastes it without a word
Rewards
+75 XP +30 Standing Saturday Market (Frank Intro) Log: "Harvest & Extraction"
Q6
Battening Down
Unlock: After Q5 — Fall Y1
"Beekeeping isn't about summer. Any fool can keep bees in July. The real work is getting them to March."
Objectives
  • Complete varroa treatment on all active hives before the first frost
  • Install mouse guards on every hive entrance
  • Verify honey stores are at least 60 lbs per hive (or feed to target)
  • Add ventilation board and entrance reducer to each hive
Rewards
+100 XP +30 Standing Lloyd Petersen Mentioned by Bob Log: "Winterization"
Q7
Lloyd's Lot
Unlock: After Q6 + Level 2 + survived first winter
"My back says I'm fifty-eight. My heart says maybe not even that. But the lot's good ground. You'll see."
Objectives
  • Meet Lloyd Petersen at his property with Bob, walk the proposed apiary site
  • Pay the $200 clearing fee to Lloyd
  • Complete 3 site-prep tasks across 1 week (brush clearing, leveling, post-setting)
  • Place the first hive at the Timber & Woodlot location
Rewards
+100 XP +40 Standing Timber & Woodlot Apiary (4 slots) Log: "Apiary Site Selection"
Q8
Spring Count
Unlock: After Q7 + second winter survived — Spring Y3
"I think you know more than I did at this point. Don't tell anyone I said that."
Objectives
  • Inspect all hives across both apiaries within a single week
  • Document winter survival rate (colonies alive vs. colonies started)
  • Identify any overwintered colonies that need immediate intervention
  • Report full findings to Bob in a structured verbal summary
Rewards
+150 XP +50 Standing Recurring Mentorship Visits (bi-weekly, permanent) Log: "The Mentor's Approval"
Q9
Bob's Corner
Unlock: Year 4 + Standing 400 + 6+ active hives
"I've got two hives left over on the east row. I can't give them the attention they need anymore. They ought to go to someone who will."
Objectives
  • Accept stewardship of Bob's 2 remaining active hives — transfer them to your apiary
  • Perform a full inspection on each within 3 days of transfer
  • Bring Bob a verbal inspection report on their status — he listens without looking
  • Keep both hives alive through the following winter
Rewards
+175 XP +40 Standing 2 Established Colonies (Bob's genetics) Log: "Taking Stewardship"
Q10
The Quiet Chair
Unlock: After Q9 + Year 5 + Bob's hives survived a second winter
"You know what I like best about sitting here now? I can hear them from the porch. They don't need me. But they're still there."
Objectives
  • Complete a full season-end inspection of all hives — Bob watches from his porch chair
  • Produce a written season summary in the journal for Bob to read at his pace
  • Bring Bob one jar of honey from the hives he started — the original surviving colony's lineage
  • Sit with Bob on the porch for the cutscene: he names the surviving colony "The One That Made It"
Rewards
+200 XP +60 Standing Bob's Apiary Tools (heirloom equipment set) Chain Complete — Emotional Anchor Unlocked Log: "What We Pass On"
NPC 02 — Post Office, Cedar Bend

June — The Postmaster

She knows everything that comes into Cedar Bend. Eventually, she knows everything about Eli.

Location: Cedar Bend Post Office Years: 1 – 4 Domain: Mail, Orders, Community Connection Rewards: Ordering Perks, Mail Unlocks, Buffs
Narrative Arc

June starts as pure function — she hands over packages. Over time she becomes curious: what are all these buzzing boxes, all this equipment? She learns the language of beekeeping through Eli's orders. By Year 3 she's quietly invested. By Year 4 she's the person in Cedar Bend who knows Eli's operation best, because she's watched it grow one package at a time.

Q1
The Buzzing Box
Unlock: Year 1, Spring — first bee package order
"Sir. Your package is... vibrating. And it is labeled 'LIVE BEES.' I'd like you to take it now. Immediately."
Objectives
  • Visit the post office to collect the spring bee package before 10 AM
  • Reassure June that the package is safe and properly ventilated
  • Transport the package to the apiary without stressing the colony
  • Install the package within the same in-game day
Rewards
+30 XP +10 Standing June unlocked as regular NPC Log: "Package Bee Installation"
Q2
Handle With Care
Unlock: After Q1 — Summer Y1 (random event trigger)
"It was in that condition when it arrived from the distribution center. I am very sorry. The bees seem... less sorry than I am."
Objectives
  • Receive a damaged bee package — the screen is partially torn on one side
  • Assess colony losses: count surviving bees and queen-cage status within 30 minutes
  • Emergency-install the surviving colony using the damaged-package protocol
  • Confirm queen release and colony establishment within 5 days
Rewards
+50 XP +20 Standing Replacement Package Credit (1x, June files claim) Log: "Emergency Package Recovery"
Q3
Catalog Season
Unlock: After Q2 + Standing 50 — Late Spring Y1

  • Place your first mail-order — any item from the extended catalog
  • Pick up the completed order when it arrives (2–3 in-game days)

Rewards
+40 XP +15 Standing Mail-Order Catalog (extended equipment list) Log: "Mail-Order Beekeeping"
Q4
The Lost Order
Unlock: After Q3 — Year 2, any season
"The tracking says it was delivered. But I have no record of it and neither does the distribution center in Des Moines. I intend to find it."
Objectives
  • A queen shipment goes missing — track it across 3 NPCs who may have seen it
  • Check the CVBA bulletin board, the diner, and Lloyd Petersen's property
  • June resolves it through the postal system (takes 4 in-game days)
  • The queen arrives late but alive — install her immediately
Rewards
+60 XP +25 Standing Priority Shipping Upgrade (orders arrive 1 day faster, permanent) Log: "Queen Transit and Survival"

Rewards
+80 XP +30 Standing 2–4 Salvage Colonies + Used Equipment Set Log: "Rescuing Abandoned Hives"

Objectives

+50 XP +20 Standing Mail-Order Honey Sales Channel (ship jars directly to buyers) Log: "Honey Shipping & Packaging"

Swarm in the Mailbox

Special Delivery

  • Give June a jar from every honey varietal you've ever produced — she keeps them on her desk
Rewards
+150 XP +50 Standing VIP Order Status (free expedited shipping, permanent) Chain Complete Log: "Four Years of Cedar Bend Honey"
NPC 03 — The Crossroads Diner

Rose — The Crossroads Diner Waitress

She's seen everyone in Cedar Bend at their worst by 7 AM. She has opinions. She is usually right.

Location: Crossroads Diner Years: 1 – 4 Domain: Food, Community, Local Commerce Rewards: Meal Buffs, Honey Sales Channel, Standing
Narrative Arc

Rose starts suspicious — another hobby farmer who will be gone by fall. Over time she watches Eli come in week after week, tired and focused. She becomes the diner's most vocal advocate for local honey. The arc ends with her naming a pie after his operation, which is the highest honor Cedar Bend can bestow.

Q1
Honey in the Coffee
Unlock: Year 1, Summer — first visit to the diner
"You brought your own honey. To a diner. You know we have sugar packets."
Objectives
  • Order coffee at the diner and offer Rose a taste of your honey alongside it
  • Rose grudgingly admits it is better than the commercial honey she stocks
  • She asks: how much do you have? Answer honestly (your current jar count)
  • Bring 2 sample jars within 3 days — she'll evaluate them for kitchen use
Rewards
+30 XP +10 Standing Rose unlocked as regular NPC Meal Discount (10% at diner, permanent)
Q2
The Daily Special

"I need eight jars before Friday. Same grade as the sample. If you show up with something dark and crystalized, we don't have this conversation again."

Rewards
+50 XP +20 Standing Weekly Diner Honey Order (guaranteed income, 8 jars/week) Log: "Commercial Kitchen Quality Standards"

NPC 04 — Cedar Bend Feed & Supply

Carl Tanner — Feed & Supply

He's stocked the same shelves for thirty years. He's never had a beekeeper as a regular customer before. He finds this interesting.

Location: Tanner's Supply, Cedar Bend Years: 1 – 4 Domain: Equipment, Supplies, Commerce Rewards: Discounts, Special Orders, Unique Equipment
Narrative Arc

Carl is a merchant first, a character second — until he isn't. He starts practically: you buy things, he sells them. Over time he begins anticipating what Eli needs before he asks. By Year 3 he's stocking items specifically because Eli might need them. The arc ends with Carl creating a permanent dedicated beekeeping section in the store, the first one in the store's history.

NPC 05 — Adjacent Property (Fence Line)

Darlene Kowalski — Neighbor & Master Beekeeper

Third-generation Cedar Bend beekeeper. Retired master. She is your standard and your safety net. She does not sugarcoat.

Location: Darlene's Property (fence line) / Crossroads Diner (Fridays) Years: 1 – 5 Domain: Queen Management, Craft Mastery, Genetics Rewards: Queen Sourcing, Premium Genetics, Equipment
Narrative Arc

Year 1: Darlene is the demonstration of what Eli could be. Year 2: She becomes a peer relationship — she trusts his judgment. Year 3: She defers to him on techniques she hasn't tried. Years 4–5: She begins the slow process of stepping back from beekeeping entirely — her hands aren't steady enough anymore. The arc ends when Eli does for her grandmother's queen line what she can no longer do herself.

Q1
Over the Fence
Unlock: Year 1, Spring — visit Darlene's property
"Come over. Watch. Don't touch anything yet. Ask questions after, not during."
Objectives
  • Visit Darlene's property and observe her inspect one of her hives

She has spent thirty years watching colonies die and trying to understand why. She is not pessimistic. She is precise.

Location: Extension Office, Cedar Bend / CVBA Meetings Years: 1 – 5 (triggered by hive count) Domain: Science, IPM, Diagnostics, Mastery Rewards: Diagnostic Tools, Lab Services, Simulation Data, Certification
Narrative Arc — Hive Milestone Progression

Dr. Harwick's 10 quests are not triggered by time or story events — they are triggered by hive count milestones. Each time Eli places another hive, she appears. She is tracking him. Each milestone she teaches something new, then reveals something personal. The arc moves from clinical interest to mentorship to legacy. She is passing down thirty years of research to a sixteen-year-old in Iowa. She has no better option. She finds this comforting.

How This Chain Works: Each quest triggers automatically when the corresponding hive milestone is reached. Dr. Harwick sends a note (via June's post office) or appears at the extension office.
