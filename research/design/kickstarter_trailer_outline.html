Section 01

Overview

A day in the life -- show the satisfying loop of tending bees, pulling honey, and building a small-town reputation.

This trailer focuses entirely on the core gameplay loop: inspecting hives, harvesting and processing honey, and selling it in town. Art assets are in development -- a pixel artist is being hired -- so the footage emphasizes functional, working systems. The main theme plays wall-to-wall as the soundtrack.

Section 02

Pre-Recording Checklist

Before you hit record, set up a save file that's in good shape:

  • Mid-spring or early summer (peak season, flowers visible, good weather)
  • A few hives with capped honey frames ready to pull
  • Smoker and bee suit in inventory
  • Some jars already bottled (so the sell screen has stock)
  • Dev mode ON for Level 5 inspection visuals (full frame detail)
  • HUD visible with weather, time, energy -- hide debug text
  • Resolution set to 1920x1080 (Kickstarter embeds at 16:9)

Walking SpeedSpeed up ALL walking/transition footage to 1.5-2x

Addressing the Art Situation

Option A (Recommended)

Add a brief text overlay early in the video: "Gameplay footage -- final pixel art in development". This is honest, common for Kickstarter game trailers, and actually works in your favor because it shows the systems are REAL and WORKING. Backers fund potential, and functional gameplay is more convincing than pretty art with no mechanics.

Option B (Minimal)

Just include "Art: [Pixel Artist] -- Coming Soon" on the closing card and let the gameplay speak for itself. If the core loop is satisfying to watch, people won't fixate on placeholder sprites.

What to Emphasize

The trailer should make viewers feel three things:

"This is real"

Functional gameplay, not a concept video. Everything the viewer sees is running in-engine right now.

"This has depth"

The inspection system, cell-level simulation, multiple minigames. This isn't a simple clicker -- every hive is a living system.

"This is cozy"

Music, pacing, seasonal world, small-town setting. The viewer should feel warm watching this.

What to Avoid

  • Don't show menus, settings, or debug output
  • Don't show long walks between locations (speed them up)
  • Don't try to explain game mechanics with text -- let the visuals do it
  • Don't show any rough edges (UI bugs, empty areas, placeholder text)
  • Don't exceed 90 seconds -- shorter is better for Kickstarter

Recording Tips

Tip Detail
ResolutionRecord at 1920x1080. Kickstarter embeds at 16:9.
Clean HUDWeather icon, time, energy bar all visible and working. Hide any debug text.
Consistent SaveUse ONE save file for all recording so inventory/money/season are consistent across shots.
Multiple TakesRecord each section 3-4 times. You want options in the edit.
Audio StrategyRecord with game audio ON for SFX reference, but replace entirely with mainTheme.mp3 in post. Mix bee_buzz_loop.wav subtly under the music during inspection.
