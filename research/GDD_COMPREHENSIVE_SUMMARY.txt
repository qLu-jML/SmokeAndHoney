EQUIPMENT CONDITION DEGRADATION
- Degrades weekly from UV, moisture, propolis, physical use
- Storage in shed with maintenance: -1/week
- Active use maintained but unsheltered: -2/week
- No protective paint: -4/week
- Near standing water/high humidity: -3/week
- Passive restore in shed: +1/week

Condition Range → Colony Impact:
- 100-80: No visible wear, no impact
- 79-50: Weathered, minor paint loss (cosmetic only, warning at 60)
- 49-30: Warped wood, gaps visible (-10% honey production, +5% small hive beetle risk)
- 29-15: Significant structural wear (-20% production, +10% pest risk, -5% winter survival)

Repair Options:

2.2 QUEEN SYSTEM
Queen is the biological engine. All colony outcomes trace back to queen quality.

Queen Properties:
- Species: Determines temperament, growth pattern, winter behavior, varroa tolerance
- Grade: S/A/B/C/D/F (overall genetic quality, degrades naturally over 2-3 years)
- Age: Tracked in seasons, laying rate declines after Year 2
- Laying Rate: Eggs per day (derived from species base modified by grade, age, season, colony health)
- Temperament: Affects inspection difficulty (defensive colonies require more smoke/experience)
- Status: Active/Failing/Missing/Virgin/Cell Present (determined by brood pattern evidence)

QUEEN SPECIES COMPARISON:
Italian:
  - Strengths: High honey production, gentle, excellent brood pattern, beginner-friendly
  - Weaknesses: Heavy spring buildup depletes stores early, less varroa-resistant

Carniolan:
  - Strengths: Frugal in winter, explosive spring buildup, very gentle, good comb builders
  - Weaknesses: Prone to swarming without proactive space management

Russian:
  - Strengths: Strong natural varroa resistance, hardy in cold
  - Weaknesses: More defensive, slower spring buildup, harder to source

Buckfast:
  - Strengths: Disease-resistant, calm, consistent honey production
  - Weaknesses: Unlockable mid-game, more expensive

Caucasian:
  - Strengths: Excellent foragers, long tongues reach more flower types
  - Weaknesses: Very slow spring buildup, susceptible to nosema

QUEEN GRADE EFFECTS:
S Grade: +25% laying rate above base → Exceptional brood pattern, fast population growth, high honey yield
A Grade: +10% above base → Solid, reliable colony, ideal working hive
B Grade: Base rate → Average performance, functional
C Grade: -15% below base → Spotty brood, slower growth, slightly lower honey output
D Grade: -35% below base → Irregular laying, visible brood gaps, colony at risk
F Grade: Failing/None → Immediate replacement needed

QUEEN AGE LAYING RATE DECLINE:
Year 1: ×1.00 multiplier (establishing, solid brood but not peak)
Year 2: ×1.05 multiplier (peak performance, most productive, maximum population)
Year 3: ×0.85 multiplier (slight decline, observant players notice smaller brood arc)
Year 4: ×0.65 multiplier (visible decline, spotty frames, most beekeepers replace)
Year 5: ×0.40 multiplier (significant failure, population decline accelerating)
Year 6+: ×0.20 multiplier (effectively failing, may stop laying)

Grade Degradation with Age:
- S-grade degrades to A after 18 months
- A degrades to B after 24 months
- Each subsequent grade drops on 12-month cycle from Year 3 onward
- Age multiplier and grade degradation stack multiplicatively

2.3 POPULATION SIMULATION
Colony population simulated at individual bee level. Every bee tracked through complete lifecycle: egg → death. Total population is emergent sum of all living bees across all stages.

THE WORKER BEE LIFECYCLE:
Stage 1 - Egg: 3 days (laid in cell by queen)
Stage 2 - Larva: 6 days (fed by nurse bees, grows rapidly)
Stage 3 - Capped brood: 12 days (metamorphosis inside sealed cell)
Stage 4 - Emergence: Newborn adult bee, takes ~3 days to develop flight muscles
Stage 5 - House bee (internal work): 12 days (ages into hive duties - cleaning, nursing, building)
Stage 6 - Transition: 3-4 days (gradual shift from internal to external duties)
Stage 7 - Forager (external work): 5-8 weeks depending on season and nectar availability (collects nectar, pollen, water)

Total Lifespan by Season:
- Spring/Summer: ~6 weeks (21 days development + 35 days adult)
- Fall: ~10-12 weeks (longer lived as colony prepares for winter)
- Winter: 4-6 months (cluster together for warmth, metabolize stored honey)

LIFESPAN & MORTALITY:
Base mortality rates (daily, chance of death for each cohort):
- Eggs/Larvae: 1% daily (disease, cannibalism by stressed colony)
- Capped brood: 0.5% daily (emerging failure, chalkbrood)
- House bee (0-12 days post-emergence): 0.3% daily (natural wear, accidents)
- House bee (12+ days old): 0.2% daily (normal aging, accidents)
- Forager (age 0-2 weeks): 1% daily (predation, exhaustion, accidents)
- Forager (age 2+ weeks): 2% daily (predation increases, metabolic exhaustion)
- Winter bee (cluster): 0.1-0.3% daily (protected indoors, eating stores)

FORAGER MORTALITY - EXACT FORMULA:
forager_mortality_rate = BASE_RATE × weather_modifier × season_modifier × hive_health_modifier

BASE_RATE: 1-2% for young foragers, 2-3% for older foragers
WEATHER_MODIFIER:
  - Sunny/calm: 1.0
  - Light wind/overcast: 1.15
  - Moderate wind: 1.35
  - Strong wind/rainy: 1.8
SEASON_MODIFIER:
  - Spring: 1.1 (allergies, predators returning, longer foraging)
  - Summer: 1.0 (baseline, good conditions)
  - Fall: 1.2 (migration season, predators fattening)
  - Winter: 0.0 (foragers don't fly, live at base rate in cluster)
HIVE_HEALTH_MODIFIER:
  - Healthy (HP > 75%): 0.85
  - Good (HP 50-75%): 1.0
  - Poor (HP 25-50%): 1.25
  - Critical (HP < 25%): 1.5

ROLE DISTRIBUTION & COLONY FUNCTION:
Worker bees fill roles based on life stage and colony needs:
- Nurses: House bees 3-12 days old, feed larvae and capped brood
- Builders: House bees 8-18 days old, draw comb, repair cells
- Cleaners: House bees 0-3 days old, clean cells for queen to lay in
- Foragers: Bees 21+ days old, collect nectar/pollen/water
- Guard bees: All ages can become guards when threatened, defend entrance

Ratio depends on colony needs and time of year. Spring buildup = more nurses. Summer = more foragers. Winter = all house bees (no foraging).

WHAT THE PLAYER SEES:
During inspections, no explicit "Age X days" numbers. Player sees:
- Visual brood pattern (eggs, larvae, capped brood percentages by frame)
- Population estimates (Strong/OK/Low/None for each role category at higher tiers)
- Health indicators (color-coded, visual cues, not raw numbers at normal play tiers)
- Laying rate inferred from consistency of brood pattern

2.4 DRONES (Male Bees)
Produced seasonally. Serve single purpose: mate with virgin queens. Consume resources, die after mating or are expelled in fall.

Drone Congregation Area (DCA) - GAME MECHANIC:
- When player breeds their own queens, virgin queens must fly to DCA for mating
- DCA is a location on map (unlocked in mid-game, located in designated apiary area)
- Player receives notification when virgin queen emerges: "Queen ready to mate. Fly her to DCA?"
- Player must walk virgin queen to DCA location, takes 2-3 in-game hours
- At DCA, interactive minigame: queen flies through space with drones, player guides (avoid obstacles, hit drones)
- Success chance based on queen genetics and player skill
- Failure = queen returns without mating, remains virgin (can retry next day)
- Successful mating = queen fertilized, returns to hive, begins laying

2.5 HIVE HEALTH SYSTEM
Overall Health Score (hidden, 0-100):
- Composite metric combining: population, brood pattern, disease presence, pest pressure, stores, equipment condition
- Player never sees this number directly
- Shown as visual state: Healthy (color), OK (amber), Poor (orange), Critical (red)
- Below 50 triggers warnings and intervention prompts

Health Factors:
- Population count: Strong = +20 points, OK = neutral, Low = -15
- Brood pattern quality: Solid = +15, Spotty = -10, None = -30
- Disease/pest presence: Varroa count, chalkbrood, nosema, etc. → -5 to -20 each
- Winter food stores: Adequate = +10, Low = -5, Critical = -20
- Equipment condition: Good (>75) = +5, Poor (<30) = -10
- Time since last inspection: <1 week = +2, >2 weeks = -5, >3 weeks = -15
- Swarm status: Queenless = -25, Queen cells present = -10

2.6 PESTS & DISEASE

VARROA DESTRUCTOR:
- Parasitic mite that weakens bees, spreads viruses
- Detected through sticky board monitoring or mite count inspections
- Threshold: 1 mite per 100 bees triggers "Varroa load HIGH" warning
- Treatment options: Formic acid, oxalic acid (winter only), Apivar strips, sugar roll mite counts

SMALL HIVE BEETLE (SHB):
- Larva tunnel through combs, foul honey
- Population controlled through beetle trap maintenance
- If untreated for 2+ weeks, can destroy brood/honey comb

CHALKBROOD:
- Fungal disease affecting capped brood
- Manifests as white mummified pupae visible on frames
- Triggers when colony is cold and damp; worse in spring
- Treatment: Improve ventilation, increase insulation, replace queen if genetics poor

NOSEMA:
- Gut parasite in adult bees
- Reduces lifespan and foraging efficiency
- Spread through fecal contamination (poor winter sanitation)
- Prevention through fall treatment with Fumagillin

AMERICAN FOULBROOD (AFB):
- Bacterial disease of larvae, devastating and incurable
- Spores persist decades in equipment
- Treatment: Destroy colony and burn equipment (game over for that hive)
- Critical teaching moment: bee biology and responsibility

EUROPEAN FOULBROOD (EFB):
- Less severe than AFB, manageable
- Visible as twisted, discolored larvae
- Treatment: Requeening with resistant genetics or antibiotic treatment

DISEASE CONTAGION - SPREAD MECHANICS:
- Diseases spread from hive to hive through: equipment sharing, robbing, drifting, poor hygiene
- If two hives at same location and one is diseased, contagion chance = 5% per day
- Sharing equipment between hives increases contagion chance
- Player learns through experience: isolation and equipment hygiene matter

2.7 HIVE TYPES
Langstroth (default): 8-frame or 10-frame. Standard configuration. All beginner mechanics designed around this.
Warré (unlockable): Vertical stacking, natural comb building. Slower honey production but excellent swarm genetics.
Top-Bar (unlockable): Horizontal design, minimal disturbance. Lower yields, but great for observation.

Each type has different frame dimensions, cell grids, and interaction mechanics.

2.8 FRAME INSPECTION & QUEEN LAYING PATTERNS

THE BROOD NEST:
Typically centered on 3-4 frames at hive's core. Queen lays in progressively larger circles.
Pattern shows: solid eggs/larvae in center, capped brood surrounding, pollen/honey on edges.
Spotty patterns indicate queen problem or disease.

FRAME GRID SYSTEM (for game):
Deep frame: 70 cells wide × 50 cells tall = 3,500 cells per side
Medium super frame: 70 cells wide × 35 cells tall = 2,450 cells per side

Each cell can be: Empty, Egg, Larva (age 1-6), Capped brood, Nectar, Honey, Pollen, Propolis

Queen laying pattern algorithm (daily tick):
1. Queen rolls upward through brood nest frames
2. Checks for empty cells below
3. Lays egg if:
   - Cell is empty
   - Frame has nurse bees present
   - Not under stress (congestion, disease, temperature)
4. Determines laying rate based on: grade, age, season, colony health
5. Produces variable egg count: 1,000-2,000 eggs per day (healthy, peak season)

FRAME & POPULATION SIMULATION (Godot Implementation):

The core simulation is frame-by-frame cellular state tracking. Each frame contains a 2D grid of cells (70×50 for deep frames, 70×35 for supers). Every 24 in-game hours (1 tick):

1. Cell aging: Eggs progress → Larvae (stages 1-6) → Capped brood → Adult emergence
2. Nurse assignment: House bees check if larvae need feeding, consume honey to produce royal jelly
3. Queen laying: Queen lays eggs in empty cells, advancing brood pattern
4. Population updates: New adults emerge, old bees age out, mortality events processed
5. Resource updates: Honey/pollen consumption tallied, stores updated
6. Equipment wear: Condition degrades

COLONY STRESS MODIFIER - FORMULA DEFINITION:
stress_multiplier = 1.0 (baseline)

Factors that INCREASE stress (multiply upward):
- Brood box > 90% full: ×1.15 (congestion)
- NO food stores: ×1.20 (starvation risk)
- Population below colony threshold: ×1.10 (weak colony)
- Disease/pest load: ×1.25 (varroa > 5%, or chalkbrood visible)
- Queenless: ×1.50 (critical stress)
- Repeated inspections (>2 per week): ×1.15 (disturbance)

Factors that DECREASE stress (multiply downward):
- Strong nectar flow: ×0.85 (abundant food)
- Adequate space (not congested): ×0.95
- Healthy queen (A/S grade, young): ×0.90

Final laying rate = base_laying_rate × grade_modifier × age_multiplier × stress_multiplier × season_modifier

High stress can reduce laying rate by 40-60%.

2.8.B CONGESTION DETECTION - BROOD-BOUND & HONEY-BOUND:

BROOD-BOUND:
- Brood nest fills 85%+ of available frames with no empty comb below for queen
- Queen cannot find cells to lay in
- Triggers swarm preparation if not addressed within 3-5 days
- Solution: Add second brood box or remove capped brood super

HONEY-BOUND:
- Honey stores surround brood nest, leaving no space for queen to expand
- Resources are available but spatially inaccessible
- Triggers swarm or reduced laying
- Solution: Add honey super above excluder to redirect incoming nectar

Both states are detectable during inspection: "This frame shows..."frames solid brood with no empty cells" / "Brood surrounded by honey stores"

2.9 QUEEN BREEDING & REARING SYSTEM

Players can breed their own queens for genetic improvement and reducing supplier costs.

REQUIREMENTS:
- Apiary Structures unlock: Breeder Yard (requires Advanced Genetics XP track, 500+ XP)
- Colony must be queenless or have a queen marked for breeding
- Supply frame with eggs from target queen (laid within 12 hours)

PROCESS (multi-day):
Day 1: Grafting
- Player selects a frame with fresh eggs from target queen
- Moves eggs into queen cells (taller cells with royal jelly)
- Game checks for minimum viable egg count (6+ eggs)
- Stress applied to colony: -15 standing

Days 2-8: Development
- Queen cells capped on day 8
- Player cannot remove cells or they'll be damaged
- Colony is queenless (if intended for queenlessness) or setting up for requeening
- Daily tick: cells progress through larval development, capping, sealing

Day 9-10: Emergence
- First virgin queen may emerge
- Game notifies: "Virgin queen has emerged!"
- If emergence successful: virgin queen present, colony responds (workers may ball/kill if colony has laying queen)
- If emergence fails (genetic defect, poor conditions): cell dies

Virgin Queen Characteristics:
- Grade inherited from mother with ±1 letter variance (A-mother → A/B-daughter)
- Virgin cannot lay until mated
- Must be flown to DCA for mating (see §2.4)
- Lives 15-20 days if unmated (plays behavior sounds, workers may sense failure)
- After successful mating: ready to lay within 2-3 days

Requeening the Hive:
- After virgin queen has mated and laid first eggs (confirming success), old queen can be removed
- OR player can introduce mated queen from Breeder Yard supply (if kept unmated in cage)
- Old queen removal: remove her carefully or use "caged queen replacement" mechanic
- Colony accepts new queen: 50-70% success chance (depends on new queen quality and integration time)

3.4 PLAYER ENERGY SYSTEM
Energy bar = player's daily stamina resource. Prevents cramming unlimited tasks into one day.

Maximum Capacity: 100 energy (full bar at start of day after sleep)

Energy Costs by Task Category:
VERY LIGHT (1-3 energy): Reviewing notes, talking to NPCs, checking hive entrance externally, reviewing market prices
LIGHT (3-8 energy): Traveling, hive weight check, placing feeding shim, checking sticky board
MODERATE (10-18 energy): Full hive inspection (one hive), planting flower bed, sugar roll mite count, bottling honey jars
HEAVY (18-28 energy): Full inspection of multiple hives back-to-back, harvesting super (pulling, transport, uncapping)
VERY HEAVY (25-40 energy): Full honey extraction day (multiple supers), assembling hive body, splitting hive with new package

Passive drain: 1-2 energy per in-game hour for standing/moving without task

BEE STINGS - ENERGY LOSS HAZARD:
Sting probability rolled during inspection (see §4.1.1 Sting Probability Model)
Each sting costs 5-8 energy
Stings stack if multiple occur in single inspection
Bee suit worn: 80% reduction in sting probability (×0.20 multiplier)
Smoker use: 15% reduction (×0.85 multiplier)
Combined suit + smoke: 95% total reduction (base chance × 0.20 × 0.85)

Example defensive colony (25% base) with suit and smoke = 25% × 0.20 × 0.85 ≈ 4.25% per inspection step

ENERGY RESTORATION:
Full night's sleep: 100 energy (only way to fully restore, 20:00-04:00 at home)
Diner meal: 40-50 energy (once per meal period - breakfast/lunch/dinner, small cash cost)
Packed lunch (crafted): 20 energy (cheaper than diner, less effective)
Coffee (morning only, before 10:00): 15-energy burst + prevents passive drain 2 hours
Nap: 2-3 in-game hours → ~25 energy (costs time, can be done at home or in truck)

Design Note: Full day's sleep allows one major task + several supporting tasks. Ambitious players need to eat in town mid-week. This creates natural reason to go to town beyond shopping.

4.2 HIVE INSPECTION SYSTEM - COMPREHENSIVE
Weekly hive inspection is primary gameplay interaction. Player gathers data, makes observations, decides on interventions.

INSPECTION FLOW:
1. Approach: Select hive, choose "Inspect" → game checks weather/time warnings
2. Smoke: Apply smoke (amount affects calmness, over/under-smoking affects quality and stress)
3. Open Hive: Remove cover, see frame-by-frame view of interior
4. Observe: Each frame shows brood pattern, honey/pollen stores, bees, queen sighting probability
5. Note & Act: Log observations, take immediate actions (add frame, mark queen, apply treatment)
6. Close: Brief summary of findings, new knowledge log entries added

STING PROBABILITY MODEL (detailed):
Each inspection step (opening, pulling frame, observing) has chance of sting.

Base sting chance per step:
- Calm colonies: 10%
- Defensive colonies: 25%
- Varies by temperament (hard-coded per queen species)

Modifiers (multiplicative):
- Bee suit worn: ×0.20 (80% reduction)
- Smoker used pre-inspection: ×0.85 (15% reduction)
- Combined (suit + smoke): ×0.20 × 0.85 = ×0.17 (95% total reduction)
- Evening inspection (after 15:00): ×1.25 multiplier (foragers returning)

Sting energy penalty: 5-8 energy per sting (stings stack if multiple occur in one inspection)

QUEEN SIGHTING - VISUAL SEARCH (PHASE 2):
Replaced probability model with interactive visual search minigame.

When player inspects hive:
1. Probability-based visibility check: 80% chance queen is visible this inspection
2. If visible (80% pass): Queen entity spawned on her assigned frame with animated bee sprites
3. Player clicks on queen to identify her (must click the actual queen sprite among worker bees)
4. Difficulty rolled (Easy 33%, Medium 33%, Hard 34%) determines:
   - Bee density: Easy 0.33×, Medium 0.67×, Hard 1.0× normal spacing
   - Frame spacing: Easy 28px, Medium 17px, Hard 12px
   - Clustering: Easy 0.28, Medium 0.20, Hard 0.16 Gaussian spread
5. XP reward:
   - Easy: +10 XP
   - Medium: +15 XP
   - Hard: +25 XP

Visual tells for queen identification:
- Longer abdomen (~45% elongated vs. workers)
- Shorter wings (expose abdomen more)
- Fewer fuzz dots (shinier appearance)
- Wider leg splay
- Breed color affects difficulty (Italian easiest, Russian hardest)

Attendant circle:
- Queen surrounded by orbiting attendant bees
- Count scales with grade: S=11, B=7, F=0
- Attendants scale inversely with difficulty: Easy ×1.5, Hard ×0.5

Wrong click penalty: None. Red flash on wrong click, player can keep trying.

Implementation: BeeOverlay.gd manages bee entities, movement, rendering. InspectionOverlay.gd integrates overlay and handles XP on queen found.

4.3 FORAGE & FLOWER SYSTEM
Forage is foundation of honey production. Quality/diversity of forage available determines honey yield, flavor profile, bee health.

FORAGE POOL (detailed in §6 Forage Pool & Nectar Mechanic):
Each location has forage pool calculated from:
- Local flora bloom phase
- Available acreage
- Player care (planted flowers, wild management)
- Seasonal nectar calendar

Forage Pool Model:
- Per-location calculation: total_nectar_available = sum(flower_type_contribution) per location
- Flower types bloom in phases throughout season
- Forage quality affects honey grade (better forage = better honey color/flavor)
- Player can improve forage by planting flowers (home apiary) or managing wild spaces

Nectar & Pollen Interaction:
- Forage pool provides daily nectar/pollen input to hive
- High forage = rapid honey production, population growth, fewer nutrition issues
- Low forage = population stagnation, starvation risk, poor honey yield
- Seasonal variation: peak summer = 6,000+ lbs potential, early spring = 1,500 lbs potential

- Advantages: Cheap, local (can pick up), supports community
- Disadvantages: Lower quality, less reliable

Tier 2 - Regional Supplier (Midwest):
- Price: $120-150 per package
- Queen Grade: B (solid, reliable)
- Availability: Early spring, 2-week lead time
- Reliability: 85% arrive healthy, 15% arrive weak
- Advantages: Reliable genetics, good queen quality
- Disadvantages: More expensive, shipping risk

Tier 3 - Premium Supplier (Georgia, California):
- Price: $180-250 per package
- Queen Grade: A/S (excellent genetics)
- Availability: Mid/late spring, 3-week lead time
- Reliability: 95%+ arrive healthy
- Advantages: Superior genetics, very reliable, established supplier reputation
- Disadvantages: Most expensive, long lead time, may need specialized equipment

PACKAGE BEE COLONY ESTABLISHMENT:
Package = 3 lbs of bees (approx. 10,000 bees) + 1 mated queen in cage + sugar candy supply
Installation process:
1. Place package on hive with queen cage inside
2. Bees eat through candy and release queen over 2-3 days
3. Bees start drawing comb, establishing brood nest
4. Takes 21-28 days for first adults to emerge and hive to reach viable strength

COMB DRAWING SYSTEM:
Once bees are installed (package or split), they must draw wax comb on frames from foundation.
- Foundation = thin wax printed with honeycomb pattern, bees extend it into full cells
- Drawing rate: ~0.2 cells per bee per day (scales with hive population and nectar flow)
- Full frame drawing (3,500 cells): 14-21 days depending on hive strength
- Multiple supers require gradual drawing (place one super at a time, wait for 80% completion before adding next)
- Uncapped honey visible on partially drawn frames (player sees progress visually)

HIVE TOOL & GLOVES - EQUIPMENT ITEMS:
Hive tool: Metal tool with flat edge (pry frames apart, remove debris) and hook (scrape propolis)
- Required for any inspection (cannot open hive without it)
- Condition: doesn't degrade, always available
- Cost: $15 (purchased or starts in inventory)

Gloves: Protect hands from stings, allow detailed work
- Increase inspection difficulty slightly (harder to feel frame details)
- Reduce sting probability somewhat
- Cost: $8/pair, limited durability (wear out after 30-40 inspections)
- Can upgrade to longer-arm cuff versions ($12)

STORAGE CHEST SYSTEM:

- Home property (large chest, unlimited items)

Items stored are preserved between days/seasons. Useful for stockpiling supers, frames, equipment not currently needed.

4.5 QUEEN MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
Queen management is among most complex and rewarding skill areas.

Players can:
- Requeen failing/aging colonies
- Breed their own queens (mid-game unlock)
- Control swarming through reactive requeening
- Choose breeding pairs for genetic improvement

Requeening Mechanics:
1. Old queen: Locate and remove from hive
2. New queen in cage: Place cage between frames, queen inside
3. Wait 2-3 days: Workers acclimate to queen's pheromone
4. Release queen: Remove cage, workers accept (50-70% success)
5. Confirm new queen: Check for eggs within 3-5 days

Breeding Mechanics (mid-game unlock):
- See §2.9 Queen Breeding & Rearing System
- Requires Breeder Yard apiary structure
- Requires eggs from high-quality queen
- Virgin queens must fly to DCA for mating
- Success rate based on genetics and player skill

4.6 SWARM MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
Swarming is natural hive reproduction. When space is tight and resources abundant, colony prepares to split.

Swarm Triggers:
- Brood-bound condition (>3 days): Swarm impulse +25%
- Honey-bound condition: Swarm impulse +20%
- Late spring/early summer (May): Seasonal impulse baseline 10-15%
- Young, vigorous queen: +10% swarm tendency
- Poor ventilation: +15%

Swarm Detection:
- Appears as "Swarm cells visible" during inspection
- Player sees queen cells (longer, vertical, centered on frame)
- Warning notification: "This hive is preparing to swarm!"
- 7-10 day window to intervene

Swarm Interventions:
Option 1 - Add Space: Place second brood box or super to relieve congestion (-15% swarm impulse)
Option 2 - Remove Brood: Take frame of capped brood to split/nuc, reduce brood-bound pressure
Option 3 - Split Hive: Intentionally divide colony into two (controlled split vs. wild swarm)
Option 4 - Break Swarm Cells: Remove visible queen cells, reset impulse (temporary, must address root cause)

Swarm Occurrence (if not prevented):
- Hive becomes queenless (old queen leaves with swarm)
- Population drops ~50% (with departing swarm)
- Remaining bees are queenless but have queen cells
- Player can: catch swarm (uncertain), let replacement queen emerge, or requeen manually
- Game notifies: "A swarm has left the hive!" with map indicator if swarm was caught

4.7 HARVEST SYSTEM - COMPREHENSIVE

Honey harvest is primary payoff moment of year. Every decision converges into: how much honey, how good is it?

PIPELINE:
Transport super to extraction facility → Uncap frames → Spin extractor → Read moisture → Bottle → Grade → Sell

SUPER & BOX MANAGEMENT (see §2.1 and §3.1 Harvest subsection above for detailed mechanics)

HARVEST DECISION & FRAME MARKING:

Player reviews colony stores during inspection:
- Shows percentage honey per frame
- Shows capped vs. uncapped honey
- Shows adjacent to brood/pollen (can't harvest adjacent to brood during extraction - cross-contamination)

Player marks frames as "ready to harvest":
- Minimum 75% capped on frame side
- Minimum 6 frame sides available (3 whole frames)
- Warning if harvest will leave insufficient winter stores (<60 lbs honey remaining)

Harvest Readiness Indicators:
- Frame side color indicator: green (ready, >75% capped), yellow (nearly ready, 50-75%), orange (not ready, <50%)
- Summary per super: "Super 1: 4 frames ready, 2 nearly ready"
- Tooltip on each frame: "Ready to harvest" / "Not ready (too much brood)"

UNCAPPING MINI-GAME: "THE UNCAPPING SWIPE"
Purpose: Physically reveal honey cells for extraction
Mechanic: Timed swipe minigame

Setup:
- Frame positioned horizontally in uncapping tank
- Uncapping knife visible at bottom of screen
- Player controls knife with mouse (drag up frame to swipe) or gamepad

Timing:
- Window to swipe each frame: 10 seconds real-time
- Difficulty increases with frame fill percentage:
  - <50% fill: very forgiving, slow knife speed required
  - 50-75% fill: moderate, normal knife speed
  - 75-100% fill: challenging, faster knife needed

Skill Levels:
- Tool progression: basic uncapping knife (slow) → heated knife (medium) → electric knife (fast)
- Player can upgrade tools as skill/money improves
- Higher skill = faster swipe window = more honey revealed per frame

Output per Frame:
- Successful swipe: 90% of cappings removed, honey flows readily into tank
- Partial swipe: 60% cappings removed, honey loss to propolis, -10% yield
- Failed swipe (time out): frame passed to extractor with caps on → reduced yield, quality penalty

Repeat for each frame side (up to 6 frame sides per super).

EXTRACTION & GRADING:

Extraction Flow:
1. Uncapped frames loaded into centrifuge extractor
2. Extractor spun: honey forced from cells via centrifugal force
3. Spinner runs 3-5 cycles (reloading frames for second side) until frames honey level drops to ~10%
4. Honey flows through strainer into tank
5. Honey sits in settling tank for 24-48 hours (in real time, player can wait or advance to next day)
6. Moisture content measured using refractometer or humidity meter

Yield Calculation:
Base yield = frame_honey_percentage × 3.5 lbs/full-frame × number_of_sides
Example: 90% full frame (both sides) = 0.90 × 3.5 × 2 = 6.3 lbs honey

Modifiers:
- Extraction efficiency: uncapping quality affects honey loss (perfect: 95%, partial: 85%, poor: 70%)
- Extractor condition: poor equipment → -15% efficiency
- Weather: cold honey spins less efficiently (-10%)
- Beekeeper experience: higher skill → better extraction technique (+5%)

Final yield = base × efficiency × weather_modifier × skill_modifier

Moisture Calculation:
Moisture content determined by:
- Season (early harvest = higher moisture, late harvest = lower)
- Forage type (clover honey = naturally lower moisture, wildflower = higher)
- Weather during collection (humid summer = higher, dry fall = lower)
- Capping percentage (uncapped honey dries to proper moisture slower)

Moisture Grading Scale:
- Below 18%: Excellent (proper moisture, won't ferment, premium grade)
- 18-21%: Good (acceptable, standard grade, won't ferment)
- 21-24%: Fair (high side, risk of fermentation if stored warm)
- Above 24%: Poor (will ferment if stored, must sell quickly or feed to bees)

In-game, player uses simple refractometer interaction: place drop on lens, read scale. Game calculates moisture based on harvest conditions, shows result.

GRADING TABLE:

---------|---------------|-----------|----------------|--------------|------------------

BEESWAX COLLECTION:

- 6 frame sides = ~1.2 lbs cappings total per super
- Rendering: cappings melted, honey separated, yields ~0.5 lbs pure beeswax per pound cappings

Beeswax uses:

WINTER RESERVE WARNING:

Critical game mechanic: over-harvesting leaves colony without winter stores = starvation = colony death

Check on harvest:

- Confirmation message: "Harvesting will leave only X lbs honey. This colony may not survive winter without supplemental feeding. Continue?"
- Supplemental feeding: sugar syrup purchased/crafted, fed to colony in fall (expensive, impacts profit margins)

This forces player decision: squeeze more profit, or play it safe with stores. Consequence if wrong = colony death mid-winter.

4.10 WEATHER SYSTEM

Weather affects gameplay: inspection quality, foraging, overwinter conditions, bee behavior.

Weather Types:
- Sunny: Best conditions, full foraging, optimal inspections
- Partly Cloudy: Good conditions, slightly reduced foraging, acceptable inspections
- Overcast: Acceptable conditions, reduced nectar collection, acceptable inspections
- Rainy: Poor conditions, no foraging, inspections discouraged (warning), poor data
- Windy: Reduced foraging, difficult inspections, damage risk
- Cold: Cluster behavior, no foraging, limited outdoor work
- Snow: Impossible to work outdoors, hives inaccessible

Weather Transitions:
- Changes hourly with smooth visual transition
- Weekly weather pattern (generated at start of each week)
- Seasonal pattern: spring = variable, summer = mostly clear, fall = increasing rain, winter = cold/snow

Foraging Impact:
- Sunny/partly cloudy: full forage collection
- Overcast: -20% forage
- Rainy: -80% forage (bees stay home)
- Windy: -40% forage (risky to fly)
- Cold: -100% forage (bees don't fly below ~55°F)

Inspection Impact:
- Sunny/partly cloudy: 100% data visibility, optimal quality
- Overcast: 80% data visibility, acceptable quality
- Rainy: 40% data visibility, poor quality, warning shown
- Windy: 60% data visibility, moderate difficulty
- Poor weather warning: "Weather is poor for hive work. Data may be unreliable."

Winter Survival Impact:
- Heavy snow: insulation check (colonies with winter wrap survive better)
- Extreme cold (-20°F): cold clustering energy drain (increased honey consumption)
- Long cold spell (2+ weeks): survival probability check (weak colonies may not make it)
