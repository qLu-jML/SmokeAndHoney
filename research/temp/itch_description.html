One Hive Survived. The Rest Is Up to You.

Smoke & Honey is a deep, authentic beekeeping simulator set in rural Iowa in the mid-1990s — built from a lifetime of real beekeeping experience. Your uncle kept bees for thirty years. After a devastating winter, eleven of his twelve hives are dead. He calls and asks you to come help him rebuild. You create your character, arrive at the property, and find one living hive in a graveyard of empty boxes. That's the whole game. That's where it starts.

What You'll Do

Inspect your hives — Pull frames, read the comb, spot the queen, and learn what your bees are telling you. No dashboards. No stat screens. You learn by looking.

Work through the seasons — Spring buildup, summer nectar flow, fall preparation, and the long wait of winter. Every decision carries into the next season.

Harvest and sell honey — Uncap frames, spin the extractor, jar your harvest, and bring it to market. Build your reputation in the small town of Cedar Bend.

Grow your apiary — Start with one hive and expand across multiple locations, from timber edges to river bottomland to the Harmon farm.

Built by a Real Beekeeper

This is not a casual farming game with a bee mini-game bolted on. Every mechanic in Smoke & Honey is grounded in real beekeeping — real brood patterns, real varroa management, real seasonal forage cycles. Players who learn the game learn real beekeeping principles. The bees respond to actual biology. The forage pool depletes. Trees you plant in Year 1 bloom in Year 4.

This is the only game where mastering the simulation means you've actually learned beekeeping.
