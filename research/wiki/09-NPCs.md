# NPCs


NPCs are not decorative — each serves a functional role in the player's progression and provides authentic beekeeping context through their dialogue.


| NPC | Role | Relationship Arc |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Uncle Bob (Home Property NPC) | Experienced local beekeeper and family friend. Lives nearby. The player's first point of contact and primary tutorial presence. Implemented in scripts/npc/uncle_bob.gd. Stands at position (380, 140) on the home property. Interact via E key. | Year 1: Tutorial anchor. Gives 6 rotating beekeeping hints on interaction, rewarding +2 XP per talk. Speech bubble auto-dismisses via DialogueUI. Appears at the Crossroads Diner on Tuesday mornings. Expanded dialogue tree (quest lines, seasonal advice) is Year 1–2 content planned for Phase 2. |
| Darlene Kowalski (Mentor Beekeeper) | Retired master beekeeper, late 60s. Lives on the adjacent property. Third-generation Cedar Bend beekeeper. Warm, practical, no-nonsense. | Year 1: Teacher and safety net. Year 2: Peer. Year 3+: The player is now the expert and she defers to them on new techniques. |
| Frank Fischbach (Market Vendor) | Runs the Cedar Bend Saturday Market. Sells everything from sweet corn to pickles. Primary buyer for all player honey products. | Relationship improves with consistent quality and reliability. Unlocks premium shelf space, bulk orders, and county fair invitations. |
| Dr. Ellen Harwick (Extension Agent) | Cedar Bend State University Extension agent for Millhaven County. Expert in integrated pest management and Midwest apiculture. | Triggered by pest or disease events. Provides lab analysis, treatment guidance, and the game's most advanced mastery quests. |
| The Harmon Farm (Walt & Kacey) | Family corn/soybean operation on the east side. Walt Harmon rotates crops annually. His daughter Kacey studied agronomy and is more open to sustainable practices. | Walt is initially indifferent. Through quests with Kacey, the farm can shift toward cover crops and better pesticide timing, unlocking a major forage zone. |
| The Cedar Valley Beekeepers Association | Regional beekeeping association. Meets monthly at the Grange Hall in Cedar Bend. | Unlocked at Level 3. Offers queen trades, used equipment, and rare knowledge in exchange for reputation and honey donations. |
| Silas Crenshaw (Carpenter) | Semi-retired carpenter and handyman, early 70s. Runs a small workshop behind the Cedar Bend hardware store. Built half the barns in Millhaven County. Quiet, precise, opinionated about lumber quality. Knew Uncle Bob well -- built the original Honey House for him decades ago. Takes pride in his work and expects the same from others. | Year 1: Introduced through the dilapidated Honey House. Assesses the damage and agrees to rebuild if the player gathers materials and pays his labor fee. Year 1-2: Restores the Honey House through a 4-quest chain. Available afterward for structural upgrades and cosmetic building projects on the home property. His dialogue reflects a craftsman's philosophy -- he sees beekeeping as another form of building something that lasts. |
