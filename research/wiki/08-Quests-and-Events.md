# Quests & Events


### 8.1 Quest Types


| Tutorial Quests | Guided tasks in Year 1 introducing each core mechanic. Cannot fail — always provide a path forward. |
| --- | --- |
| Daily Tasks | Small weekly objectives. 'Inspect Hive 2 this week.' 'Harvest at least 3 frames.' Low stakes, steady XP. |
| Seasonal Goals | 'Overwinter all hives.' 'Achieve a honey yield of X this summer.' Higher stakes, major XP and unlock rewards. |
| NPC Quests | Each NPC has a multi-season quest line. Completing them unlocks reputation, new dialogue, unique items, and story. |
| Challenge Events | Unexpected events the player must respond to. Not all are negative — some are opportunities. |
| Mastery Quests | Late-game deep dives into advanced techniques: queen rearing, varietal honey production, mead aging, etc. |


#### Quest Reward Structure


| Quest Type | XP Reward | Standing Reward | Other Rewards | Failure Penalty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tutorial Quest | 25 XP each | +15 | Starter tools (smoker, veil, hive tool) on completion of first three quests | None — cannot fail |
| Daily Task | 10–30 XP | +3 | Cash bonus $5–25 based on difficulty | None (miss the reward only) |
| Seasonal Goal | 100–200 XP | +20 to +50 | Cash bonus $50–200; may unlock a structure or rare item | −10 Standing, no XP |
| NPC Quest | 75–150 XP | +30 to +75 (with that NPC; +15 global) | Unique items: rare queen voucher, special recipe, equipment upgrade, story progression | −15 Standing with that NPC; questline delayed one season |
| Challenge Event (survived) | 50–100 XP | +10 to +20 | Varies — AFB survived: mentor dialogue, diagnostic skill boost; Swarm caught: free colony | No additional penalty beyond the event consequence itself |
| Mastery Quest | 200 XP | +50 | Premium tier unlock, advanced technique, or prestige recognition from NPCs | −20 Standing; quest resets after one season cooldown |


### 8.2 Seasonal Events


| Event | Season | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |

### 8.3 Challenge Events

Challenge events are unexpected disruptions that require a player response. They scale in severity with the player's experience level.


| Event | Trigger | Player Response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Varroa Outbreak | Mite count spikes in late summer due to regional spread | Test all hives, apply treatment within 2 weeks or winter losses increase significantly |
| Queen Failure | An A-grade queen drops to D unexpectedly | Inspect to confirm, order replacement queen, manage the colony through the gap |
| Unexpected Cold Snap | Late spring frost event | Check brood survival, consider emergency feeding, assess for chilled brood |
| Robbing Event | A strong hive raids a weaker neighbor during dearth | Reduce entrances, separate hives, identify and address the dearth condition |
| AFB Scare | Suspicious brood discovered in an inspection | Perform ropiness test, contact mentor NPC, decide on destruction vs. treatment (where legal) |
| Swarm Escaped | Swarm issued from a hive the player failed to manage | Check remaining colony for a laying queen. Optionally chase the swarm for recovery. |
| Pesticide Event | Neighboring farm applies chemicals during bloom | Find dead bees at the entrance. Report to the farmer NPC. Colony health temporarily reduced. |
| Winter Loss | A hive does not survive winter | Spring inspection reveals dead colony. Clean hive. Assess cause. Plan replacement. |


### 8.4 NPC Quest Chains

Each NPC has a multi-quest chain that spans multiple seasons and progression levels. Quest chains interlock — completing one NPC's quests often unlocks or triggers quests for another. 38 total NPC quests provide 3,850 XP (roughly 16% of the 24,000 XP needed for Master Beekeeper). The remaining 84% comes from the steady rhythms of beekeeping itself.

#### Quest Chain Overview


| NPC | Total Quests | Level Range | Seasons | Major Unlock |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Uncle Bob | 8 (chain) + recurring mentorship | 1–3 | Year 1–Year 3 | Timber & Woodlot apiary (Level 2) |
| Darlene Kowalski | 6 (chain) + seasonal advice | 1–4 | Year 1–Year 4 | Queen genetics knowledge, emergency queen sourcing |
| Frank Fischbach | 5 (chain) + weekly market | 1–4 | Year 1–Year 4 | Premium shelf space, bulk orders, county fair entry |
| Dr. Ellen Harwick | 5 (chain) + lab services | 2–5 | Year 2–Year 5 | Advanced diagnostics, mastery quests, IPM certification |
| Walt & Kacey Harmon | 6 (chain through Kacey) | 2–4 | Year 2–Year 4 | Harmon Farm / Orchard apiary location |
| Cedar Valley BKA | 4 (chain) + monthly meetings | 2–4 | Year 2–Year 4 | River Bottom location (Level 3 + Standing 500) |
| Silas Crenshaw (Carpenter) | 4 (chain) | 1–2 | Year 1–Year 2 | Honey House restoration, structural upgrades |


**Design Note — Quest Pacing:**

No NPC dumps their full quest chain at once. Quests are gated by level, season, standing, and prerequisite completion. A player who focuses only on one NPC will hit natural walls that push them toward other characters.

**Design Note — Bob's Recurring Presence:**

After the chain completes, Bob doesn't vanish. He remains available for bi-weekly visits (+20–25 XP), appears at the Crossroads Diner on Tuesdays, and has seasonal dialogue that reflects his health and the weather. He stops being the quest driver and becomes the emotional anchor.

**Design Note — Silas and Pacing:**

The Honey House restoration is designed to complete by mid-summer Year 1, just in time for the first major honey harvest. Players who delay the quest chain can still harvest using manual equipment (comb scraper, hand spinner, bottling kit purchased from the catalogue), but the process is slower, uses more energy, and produces slightly lower-quality results. The Honey House is the carrot; manual processing is the stick.

**Design Note — XP Balance:**

3,850 XP from all NPC quests represents roughly 16% of the 24,000 XP needed for Master Beekeeper. NPC quests provide structure and narrative. The bees provide the progression.

**Design Note — Failure as Progression:**

Quest failures don't lock the player out permanently. A failed seasonal goal delays progression by one season and costs standing, but the quest reappears. NPC quest failures delay the chain by one season and reduce standing. The design ensures a path forward always exists — just a slower one. This mirrors real beekeeping: a bad year sets you back, but it doesn't end you.
