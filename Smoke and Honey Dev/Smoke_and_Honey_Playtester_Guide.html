Section 01

Welcome, Playtester

Thank you for testing Smoke & Honey. You're playing an early build of a beekeeping simulation set in Cedar Bend, a small fictional town in Iowa. You've just inherited your Uncle Bob's last surviving hive after a brutal winter killed eleven of his twelve colonies. Your job is to learn the hive, keep her alive, and figure out what it means to be a beekeeper.

This guide walks you through the entire first in-game year, quest by quest. The game teaches you everything through Uncle Bob's dialogue, but if you get stuck or want to understand the bigger picture, this is your reference.

What we're testing: The full Year 1 play loop -- can you go from arrival to winterization in one unbroken flow? Does the quest chain guide you clearly? Does the pacing feel right? Are there dead spots where nothing happens? Does the simulation make sense?

Section 03

Calendar & Seasons

Cedar Bend runs on an 8-month calendar with 28 days per month (224 days per year). Each month has a name that reflects the rhythm of the land. You arrive on Day 43 -- the 15th of Greening -- when the dandelions are at peak bloom.

Spring

Quickening (1-28) · Greening (29-56)

Colony builds up on dandelion flow. Queen ramps laying. You arrive mid-Greening.

Summer

Wide-Clover (57-84) · High-Sun (85-112)

Peak nectar flow. Clover and wildflowers fill the supers. Harvest time.

Fall

Full-Earth (113-140) · Reaping (141-168)

Goldenrod bloom, then decline. Mite treatment window. Feed if stores are short.

Winter

Deepcold (169-196) · Kindlemonth (197-224)

Colony clusters. Don't open the hive. Check weight. Wait for spring.

Time pacing: Each in-game day passes in real time while you're awake. Sleep in the bed inside your house to advance to the next morning. If the quest system tells you to wait, sleep through the days -- the hive simulation runs every night while you rest.

Section 04

Year 1 Walkthrough

The first year is structured as six quests that chain together automatically. Each one teaches a core beekeeping skill through a concrete task. Uncle Bob guides you through everything -- talk to him whenever you're unsure what to do next.

"Your job is to learn this hive. Learn what she needs. Keep her going."

Quest 00
Meet Uncle Bob
Mid-Spring (Greening) · Day 43+

You've just arrived in Cedar Bend. Uncle Bob is out by his surviving hive. This quest is the gatekeeper -- nothing else in the game unlocks until you complete it. Bob explains the situation: his hospital stay, the winter that killed his hives, and why the dandelions blooming outside are the most important thing happening right now.

What to do

  1. Walk outside to where Uncle Bob is standing near the hive.
  2. Press E to talk to him. Listen to the full conversation.
  3. He'll teach you: how to open the storage chest, drag items to your toolbar, use the smoker, use the hive tool, and open your journal.
  4. The quest completes automatically after the conversation ends.

Tip: After Bob's conversation, open the storage chest (press E) and drag the smoker and hive tool to your toolbar. You'll need both for the next quest.

Quest 01
First Light
Mid-Spring (Greening) · After Q0

Your first hive inspection. Bob wants to know what's happening inside the survivor. You need to smoke the hive first (to calm the bees), then open it with the hive tool and look at least 3 frames.

What to do

  1. Select the smoker on your toolbar (mouse wheel or number keys).
  2. Walk to the hive and press E. You'll see a notification: "Hive smoked -- bees calmed!"
  3. Switch to the hive tool on your toolbar.
  4. Press E on the hive again to open the inspection view.
  5. Use A and D to navigate through at least 3 different frames.
  6. The quest completes when you've viewed 3 or more unique frames.
  7. Close the inspection with ESC.
  8. A prompt will remind you to go talk to Uncle Bob for a debrief.

Stings: If you inspect without smoking first, there's a higher chance of getting stung. Each sting costs 5-8 energy. Wearing gloves (grab them from the chest) reduces sting chance by 80%. Always smoke before you inspect.

Quest 02
Reading the Room
Spring (Greening) · After Q1 debrief

Bob wants a thorough inspection this time. You need to check at least 8 frame sides -- that means flipping frames over, not just clicking through them. This teaches you to really look at what's happening in the hive.

What to do

  1. Smoke the hive (smoker + E), then open with hive tool.
  2. Navigate through frames with A / D.
  3. Press F to flip each frame and see the back side.
  4. View at least 8 unique frame sides (4 frames, both sides each).
  5. The quest completes automatically when you hit 8 sides viewed.
  6. Close and return to Bob for the debrief.

What to look for

Bob's debrief will explain these, but while you're in the hive: the brood pattern is the oval of capped cells in the center of each frame. A tight, solid oval means the queen is laying well. Honey is stored in arcs above and around the brood. If you see empty cells scattered through the brood, that could be a problem later.

Tip: The progress counter in the inspection header shows how many sides you've examined out of the total. Aim for thorough coverage -- it's good practice for real inspections later.

Quest 03
The Girls Are Building
Late Spring (Greening) · After Q2 debrief

The dandelion flow has been fueling the colony, and the brood box is getting full. If the bees run out of room, they'll swarm -- half the colony will leave. You need to add a honey super on top to give them space.

What to do

  1. Open the storage chest and drag a honey super to your toolbar.
  2. Walk to the hive with the hive tool selected.
  3. Open the hive and use the Hive Management interface to add the super on top of the brood box.
  4. The quest completes when the super is successfully added.
  5. Return to Bob for the debrief.

Bob will explain what happens next: the dandelions will fade, but clover is coming. Wide-Clover month (summer) is when the real nectar flows and the supers fill up with harvestable honey.

The waiting period: After adding the super, there's intentional downtime before the next quest. First Pull is month-gated to Wide-Clover (summer). Use this time to check on the hive every few days, explore Cedar Bend, and meet the neighbors. Sleep through days when there's nothing to do -- the simulation advances overnight.

Quest 04
First Pull
Summer (Wide-Clover, Day 57+) · After Q3

The clover flow has filled your supers. It's harvest time. You need to find frames that are at least 80% capped, pull them, uncap, extract, and bottle the honey. This is the payoff for everything you've done so far.

What to do

  1. Open the hive and navigate to the super (press W to move up from brood box).
  2. Look for frames with 80% or more capped honey. The capping indicator in the header will tell you.
  3. Use Hive Management to pull the full super when it's ready.
  4. Take the super to the Harvest Yard area.
  5. Uncap, extract, and bottle the honey.
  6. Bring a jar to Bob. Return to Bob for the debrief.

Don't pull green honey. Frames under 80% capped contain honey with too much moisture -- it will ferment. Let the bees finish capping before you harvest. Patience pays.

After the debrief, Bob introduces you to Frank at the Saturday Market, where you can sell your honey. The market runs every Saturday -- bring jars and sell them for cash.

Quest 05
Battening Down
Fall (Full-Earth, Day 113+) · After Q4

This is the most important quest of the year. Winter is coming, and your job is to make sure the colony survives it. You'll check mite levels, treat if needed, and ensure the hive has at least 60 pounds of stores. If they're short, you'll spend your harvest money on sugar to feed them.

What to do

  1. Get the wash kit from the storage chest (or buy one from Tanner's if needed).
  2. Open the hive and run an alcohol wash to check the varroa mite count.
  3. If the count is above 3 mites per hundred bees, treat with oxalic acid.
  4. Check honey stores. If below 60 pounds, go to Tanner's Feed & Supply and buy sugar.
  5. Install a feeder bucket with 2:1 thick syrup (two parts sugar, one part water).
  6. The quest completes when mites are controlled and stores are adequate.
  7. Return to Bob for the final debrief of the year.

Bob's debrief is the longest and most important. He teaches you:

Fall feeding (2:1 thick syrup): Heavy syrup for building winter stores. The bees convert it quickly and cap it for long-term storage. This is what you do now.

Spring feeding (1:1 thin syrup): Light syrup that mimics a nectar flow. Gets the queen laying early. You'll do this in Year 2 if dandelions are late.

Winter rules: Don't open the hive. Check the weight from outside. If it feels light mid-winter, put fondant on top of the frames. Wait for spring.

Winter -- The Waiting

After Battening Down is complete, there are no more quests until Year 2. The colony clusters inside the hive for warmth. Your only job is to not open it. Check the weight occasionally. Sleep through the cold months. The simulation keeps running.

"Beekeeping isn't about summer. Any fool can keep bees in July. The real work is getting them to March."

Section 08

Giving Feedback

The most useful feedback tells us what happened, what you expected, and how it felt. We're especially interested in:

Pacing

Were there long stretches with nothing to do? Did quests come too fast? Did you understand what to do next at every point?

Clarity

Did Uncle Bob's instructions make sense? Could you figure out the controls? Were there moments of confusion about what the game wanted?

Feel

Did the hive inspection feel interesting or tedious? Was the sting system fair? Did selling honey at market feel rewarding? Did winter feel like an appropriate rest or a boring gap?

Bugs

Anything that broke, softlocked, displayed incorrectly, or behaved unexpectedly. Screenshots and steps to reproduce are gold.
