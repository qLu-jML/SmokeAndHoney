Context
The first full in-game year (8 months, 224 days) needs to be playable spring-to-spring. The simulation engine, harvest pipeline, economy, weather, and core UI all work. What's missing is the connective tissue: quest spine, disease monitoring/treatment, knowledge journal, seasonal dialogue, and winter activities.
North star: A cozy sim fan plays Quickening to Kindlemonth, feels the seasonal rhythm, harvests honey, sells it at market, prepares for winter, and understands why their hive survived or didn't.
Core interaction model: Item-based, not keybind-based. Players must carry the right tools in inventory to perform actions. This creates meaningful inventory management decisions (what do I bring to the apiary today?).

WP2: Scene-Wide Feeder System
Why: Feeding is a scene-level survival tool, NOT per-hive. All hives draw from a shared bucket daily.
Files to create: scripts/world/feeder_bucket.gd + scenes/world/feeder_bucket.tscn
Design:

Player places from inventory (uses ITEM_SYRUP_FEEDER item)
syrup_remaining: float starts at 5.0 liters when filled with ITEM_SUGAR_SYRUP
Daily: all hives on scene draw proportionally. When empty, stops. Player must refill.
Bees store feed as feed_stores (new field in HiveSimulation), NOT as sellable honey
Harvest pipeline flags/rejects frames with feed-honey content
Useful in spring (weak colony) and fall (winter prep). Not useful when nectar flows.

WP3: Alcohol Wash Mite Monitoring Mini-Game
Why: Real-world alcohol wash procedure as a mini-game, taught by mentor NPC.
Item-based trigger: Player must have "Alcohol Wash Kit" item in inventory. During inspection, if kit is present, an "Use Wash Kit" option appears in the inspection UI (button, not keybind). Bought at feed supply after Ellen teaches the technique.
Tutorial moment (FIRST TIME ONLY):
During the player's first wash (guided by mentor), Step 1 is scripted:

Player selects a brood frame. The queen happens to be on it.
Player proceeds to scoop bees into the jar
The mentor STOPS them: "Hold on! See that bee with the painted dot? That's your queen. You almost killed her. ALWAYS check for the queen before you scoop."
This teaches the lesson through consequence, not a tooltip
All subsequent washes have a "Check for Queen" step where player must visually scan the frame

Mini-game steps (after tutorial):

Frame Selection -- Pick brood frame. Must pass queen check (scan frame, confirm queen absent)
Scoop Bees -- Fill jar to ~300 bees (press E, fill gauge)
The Wash -- Add alcohol, shake (hold E, progress bar, ~5 real seconds)
Count -- Mites appear on white background. Player clicks/counts them.
Results -- Math shown: "X / 300 = Y per 100". Color-coded threshold.

Files to create: scripts/ui/alcohol_wash_overlay.gd
Files to modify:

WP4: Disease Treatment Application
Why: After monitoring, player needs to treat. Item-based, not keybind.

Player clicks "Use Treatment" button during inspection

WP5: AFB System (Rare, High-Consequence)
Why: AFB is extremely rare but devastating. Triggered by specific events, not random.
Two-State Model:

State 1 (Sub-clinical): Spores present, invisible. Triggered by: robbing dead hive, contaminated equipment, feeding non-apiary honey. Strong colony can clear (15%/week). Weak colony progresses in 4-6 weeks.
State 2 (Clinical): Visible during inspection. Sunken cappings, dark cells, foul smell notification on hive open. Scattered brood.

Rope test: Item-based. Player needs a "matchstick" or small tool item. During inspection, if AFB cells visible, "Test Suspicious Cell" button appears. Positive result = ropy, stringy pull animation.
Player response:

"Destroy Hive" button in hive_management (correct: burn hive, lose everything, protect apiary)
Do nothing = disease spreads, colony dies, robbing spreads to other hives
Talk to Ellen = diagnosis confirmation + guidance

Base probability: 1% per hive per year. Multiplied by: high varroa (2x), dead hive nearby (5x), weak colony (2x).
Files to modify:

scripts/core/HiveSimulation.gd -- AFB two-state model, spore tracking
scripts/simulation/CellStateTransition.gd -- AFB progression
scripts/ui/InspectionOverlay.gd -- Rope test button (item-gated)
scripts/ui/hive_management.gd -- "Destroy Hive" button
